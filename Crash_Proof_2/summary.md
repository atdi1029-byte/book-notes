# Crash Proof 2.0: How to Profit From the Economic Collapse
**Peter D. Schiff** | 2009 | 364 pages

## Summary

Crash Proof 2.0 is a revised edition of Schiff's 2007 book, updated after the
2008 financial crisis to show he was right about the collapse — while arguing the
real collapse is still ahead. Written from an Austrian economics framework,
Schiff diagnoses America's structural rot and offers a concrete three-part
investment survival plan: foreign stocks, gold, and liquidity.

## Core Thesis

America built a fake economy on debt, consumption, and money printing.
The dollar is headed for collapse because:
1. The US spends $800B/yr more than it earns globally (trade deficit)
2. Foreign central banks recycle those dollars back into Treasuries
   (a circular, unstable system — the "float")
3. The Federal Reserve responds to every crisis by creating more money
4. Americans save nothing and consume everything, producing nothing
5. The US government has ~$50+ trillion in unfunded liabilities it can
   only meet with the printing press

When foreign creditors stop recycling dollars, the collapse comes:
dollar falls, inflation surges, living standards plunge.

## Key Arguments by Chapter

**Ch. 1 — The Fake Economy:** GDP growth driven by consumption and
debt is not real. The 2000s boom was entirely borrowed from foreign
savings. A service economy cannot sustain prosperity.

**Ch. 2 — The Trade Deficit:** Every dollar of trade deficit is a claim
on future American assets. The "float" — foreign central banks holding
dollars to stabilize their currencies — is why the US has gotten away
with deficits for decades. When the float unwinds, the dollar collapses.

**Ch. 3 — History of Money:** Commodity money arose to solve barter
problems. Gold standard enforced fiscal discipline. Fiat money (since
1971) removed the restraint and enabled unlimited money printing.
Bretton Woods was the intermediate step — dollar as "good as gold" until
Nixon closed the gold window.

**Ch. 4 — The Housing Bubble:** Created by the Fed (cheap money),
government policy (Fannie/Freddie, CRA, tax breaks), and Wall Street
securitization. When the bubble popped, it triggered the 2008 crisis —
but the underlying dollar problem remains unresolved.

**Ch. 5 — The Stock Market:** US stocks were overvalued on real metrics.
P/E ratios at historically high levels. Dividend yields near zero. Tech
bubble was one manifestation; the structural overvaluation continues.

**Ch. 6 — Economic Statistics:** Government systematically understates
inflation (hedonics, substitution, geometric weighting), overstates GDP
(services inflation counted as growth), and manipulates unemployment
figures. Real economic conditions are worse than reported.

**Ch. 7 — Consumer Debt:** Americans borrowed to consume, not produce.
Social Security is a legal Ponzi scheme ($6.8T unfunded). FDIC creates
moral hazard. Securitization turned consumer debt into a global time bomb.
"Borrowing to produce is how poor countries become rich. Borrowing to
consume is how rich countries become poor."

**Ch. 8 — Foreign Stocks:** Move money out of dollar-denominated assets
into conservative, dividend-paying foreign stocks. Target ~8% annual yield.
Focus on Canada, Australia, Asia, Europe. Avoid ADRs, mutual funds,
Pink Sheets. Execute directly on foreign exchanges. If wrong: still win
on dividends and currency. If right: enormous gains. The 1970s is the
historical template — foreign investors tripled their money as the dollar
lost two-thirds of its value.

**Ch. 9 — Gold:** Gold was money for 4,500 years. The 1980-2000 bear
market in gold was an anomaly caused by false confidence in central
bankers. Gold is undervalued — no monetary premium reflected. Demand
will explode: remonetization, central bank buying, short squeeze, Wall
Street rediscovery, private gold standards via internet/debit cards.
Dow-to-gold ratio should return to 1:1 from 17:1. Price targets: $5,100
(20x move from $255 low), possibly $12,000+ (1:1 Dow ratio).

**Ch. 10 — Liquidity:** Keep 3-6 months expenses in USD. Rest in:
foreign currency CDs, Merk Hard Currency Fund, foreign govt bonds,
physical gold/silver (counts as liquidity). Convert all adjustable debt to
fixed. Borrow against real estate at 6.5% → invest at 8% foreign yield
for positive carry. Offshore assets protect against potential confiscation.

## Investment Framework (Three-Part Strategy)

1. **Foreign stocks** — conservative, high-dividend equities in Canada,
   Australia, Hong Kong, Singapore, Japan, Europe. Direct exchange
   execution. 10-20 stocks, 8% target yield. No ADRs, no mutual funds.

2. **Gold & silver** — physical bullion (Perth Mint), gold ETFs, mining
   stocks (40% seniors / 30% mid-tier / 20% juniors / 10% exploration).
   10-30% of total portfolio. Silver as cheaper alternative with more
   volatility.

3. **Liquidity** — foreign currency cash, junk silver coins, short-term
   foreign government bonds. Emergency fund + dry powder for
   bargain-buying during transition period.

## Austrian School Framework

Schiff is explicitly Austrian: von Mises (Human Action), Hayek (Road to
Serfdom), Rothbard (America's Great Depression) are his intellectual
foundation. Key Austrian concepts throughout:
- Malinvestment (misallocation of capital caused by artificially low rates)
- Business cycle theory (Fed creates boom → bust)
- Sound money (gold as the only real restraint on government)
- Say's Law (supply creates demand — production precedes consumption)
- Price signals (government interference distorts them)
- Spontaneous order (free markets allocate better than central planners)
