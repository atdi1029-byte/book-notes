# Stocks for the Long Run — Jeremy Siegel (6th Edition, 2022)

## One-Sentence Summary
Over two centuries of data prove that diversified stock portfolios consistently outperform bonds, gold, and cash over long holding periods, making equities the foundation of any serious wealth-building strategy.

## Key Thesis
Stocks have delivered remarkably stable real returns of 6-7% annually across every major period of American history — through wars, depressions, panics, and technological revolutions. This "equity premium" over bonds isn't an accident; it's a structural necessity of capitalism. The longer your time horizon, the more stocks dominate every other asset class in both return and risk-adjusted performance.

## Core Arguments
- $1 invested in stocks in 1802 would be worth $54.2 million by 2021; the same dollar in bonds: $50,206
- Over any 20-year period in 220+ years, stocks have never delivered a negative real return
- Mean reversion in stock returns means long-term risk is actually LOWER than bonds for 30-year horizons
- Most individual stocks are losers — only 4% beat the market; diversification through index funds is essential
- Value investing works in the long run, though it suffered an unprecedented 13.5-year drought (2006-2021)
- Over 90% of actively managed funds underperform their index over 10+ years
- GDP growth and stock returns are negatively correlated across countries — growth doesn't guarantee investment returns

## Who Should Read This
Long-term investors, anyone building a retirement portfolio, finance students, and anyone skeptical about whether stocks really are the best wealth-building vehicle. Siegel provides the definitive empirical case with 220 years of data.
