### Writing Philosophy & Approach

> It is impossible to go very deep into an idea when you run a business, no matter the number of hours the occupation entails—simply put, unless you are insensitive, the worries and feelings of responsibility occupy precious cognitive space.

- Author freed himself from business obligations, routines, and pressures during peripatetic period when book was written
- Preference for dilapidated but elegant cafés in regular neighborhoods, unpolluted with persons of commerce
- Spent much time in Heathrow Terminal 4, absorbed in writing to point of forgetting allergy to strained businessmen
- Book written during period of freedom to engage with ideas systematically, highly disciplined, well-engineered approach to gain exposure to high-impact rare events without direct business involvement

### Intellectual Disagreement as Method

- Learning most from people one disagrees with (rare practice, though Montaigne encouraged it half millennium ago)
- Detractors identified slightest cracks and revealed weaknesses of own theories
- Learned tricks from public debates, correspondence, discussions with **Robert C. Merton, Steve Ross, Myron Scholes, Philippe Jorion**, and dozens of others
- Last new counterargument to Black Swan ideas heard in 1994 (from **Elie Ayache**)
- Over career, read more material from disagreed with than from those sharing opinions
- Read more Samuelson than Hayek, more Merton (younger) than Merton (elder), more Hegel than Montaigne, more Descartes than Sextus
- Duty of every author to represent ideas of adversaries faithfully

### Author Biography (Nassim Nicholas Taleb)

- Devoted life to immersing self in problems of **luck, uncertainty, probability, and knowledge**
- Three high-profile careers around these ideas: man of letters, businessman-trader, university professor
- Spends most time as flâneur meditating in cafés across the planet
- Currently **Distinguished Professor at NYU Polytechnic Institute** and principal of **Universa LP**
- Books *Fooled by Randomness* and *The Black Swan* published in 31 languages
