# The Theory of Money and Credit

**Ludwig von Mises** · 1912 (English edition 1934, expanded 1953) · ~395 pages

## One-line
The foundational Austrian treatise that derives money from individual
subjective valuation, exposes credit expansion as the engine of the
business cycle, and argues that sound money requires redeemable gold
plus frozen fiduciary media.

## Why it matters
This is the book that put money on the same footing as every other
economic good. Before Mises, monetary theory sat in a separate
compartment — supply and demand explained the prices of wheat and
shoes, but the value of money was treated as a macro aggregate,
determined by quantity equations divorced from individual choice.
Mises closed the circle. He showed that money's purchasing power is
just the cumulative result of individuals' decisions to hold cash,
and that those decisions are governed by the same subjective value
theory Menger used for any other good. From this one unification,
everything else in Austrian macro follows.

## Core arguments
1. **Money's value is subjective.** There is no separate "macroeconomic"
   theory of money. Its purchasing power comes from individual demand
   for cash balances, valued on the margin, exactly like any other good.
2. **The regression theorem.** Money's subjective value can be traced
   back historically to a point where the money-good had non-monetary
   use-value (e.g., gold as ornament). This answers the circularity
   problem in marginal-utility theory applied to money.
3. **Money-substitutes and fiduciary media.** Claims to money fall into
   two categories: fully covered (warehouse receipts, money-certificates)
   and uncovered (fiduciary media — bank notes and deposits in excess
   of reserves). Only the second set expands the effective money supply.
4. **Credit expansion causes the cycle.** Banks issuing fiduciary media
   push the money rate of interest below the natural rate, triggering
   malinvestment in higher-order goods. The boom is literally capital
   consumption disguised as prosperity. The bust is not a failure of
   the market — it is the market correcting an earlier distortion.
5. **Sound money is not optional.** Any monetary system that permits
   discretionary fiduciary issuance is unstable. The gold standard is
   not perfect but it binds the hands of governments and banks. Mises'
   reconstruction plan: redeemable gold, frozen fiduciary media, no
   further credit expansion.

## Where it fits
Written in 1912 when Mises was 31 — before Keynes, before the Great
Depression, before Bretton Woods. The cycle theory anticipated Hayek's
*Prices and Production*, the critique of the state theory of money
anticipated the entire debate over central bank independence, and the
monetary reconstruction plan presaged the 100% reserve proposals of
Rothbard and the Chicago Plan. Everything in modern Austrian economics
is downstream of this book.
