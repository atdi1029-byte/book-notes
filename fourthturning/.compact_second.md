## Toward the Second Turning

- 1963: **Stephen Spender** characterized postwar years as **The Struggle of the Modern**—only 18 years separated America from WWII memory
- **Robert Frost** heralded new Augustan Age at Kennedy inaugural; died 1963 without witnessing what lay ahead
- Generational archetypes shifted mood from dark urgency toward energy and optimism—but this could not last indefinitely
- Each generation could sustain its role through one life phase only; none showed signs of accepting predecessor's role at like age
- Spark came **November 22, 1963**—but the saeculum would have carved its path regardless

> "The Second Turning would have come, one way or another. It was time."

## Generational Archetypes in Historical Cycles

### Heroes in Elderhood
- **Aging Heroes** embodied optimism and teamwork when younger generations spurned these qualities
- **Cotton Mather** felt his patience "tried by the contempt" of young zealots
- **Jefferson despaired** that sacrifices of 1776 would be "thrown away by the unwise and unworthy passions of their sons"

### Artists in Midlife
- **Indecisive Artists** apply expertise and process, carve a **mediator's role** between Prophet inner mission and Hero outer mission
- **Edward Wigglesworth**: "studied to preserve a due moderation"
- Instinctive moderation kept Awakenings from deteriorating into chaos

### Prophets in Young Adulthood
- Challenge moral failure of elder-built institutions, spark spiritual awakening
- **Gilbert Tennent** denounced happiness in wealth: "driving, driving to duty, duty!"
- **Benjamin Franklin**: "It seemed as if all the world were growing religious"
- **William Lloyd Garrison** vowed "to wake up a nation slumbering in the lap of moral death"

### Nomads in Childhood
- Left underprotected during social convulsion, acquire early self-discovery and cynicism
- **Cavaliers marked the greatest one-generation backstep** in education and life expectancy in American history

## G.I.s Entering Elderhood

- **LBJ's 1964 landslide** trampled Lost resistance; by 1966, G.I. Congress spent nearly as much on education/health in one year as earlier Congresses throughout all history
- **Apollo program**: Eric Hoffer called these achievements **"the triumph of the squares"**
- **Generation Gap**: G.I. edifices required beneficiaries to behave "squarely," but younger generations rejected this bargain
- **Vietnam**: neither troops nor home front behaved as G.I.s expected; **Bundy-Rusk-Rostow-McNamara "controlled-response" strategy culminated in controlled defeat**
- **Watergate** became the Awakening's Thermidor; Nixon's resignation sapped power from national government
- **AARP** emerged as "the single most powerful lobbying bloc on the planet"
- G.I. seniors became **least poor generation** while child poverty skyrocketed—transfer payments towered 10-to-1 over Great Society programs
- Millions retired into **Sun City** seniors-only communities; **first elders to call themselves "senior citizens"**
- **Politically and economically powerful yet invisible in realm of culture and values**—formative impact on youth nearly nil

## Silent Entering Midlife

- **Kennedy's assassination** left larger mark on Silent than any other generation
- **Martin Luther King Jr.** and **Robert Kennedy** deaths: "the two best they had to offer"
- **Most upwardly mobile generation in American history**; yet outer good fortune masked inner turmoil
- **Tom Wolfe** admired them as "right stuff" (Neil Armstrong); **Howard Junker**: "grown up just as the world's gone teen-age"
- **Tensions made them America's greatest generation of songwriters, comedians, and therapists**
- **Sexual revolution** hit Silent at awkward phase; from 1969-1975, **no-fault divorce laws jumped from zero to forty-five**
- **Gail Sheehy's** subjects: "somebody would let me be what I am, tender sometimes, and dependent, too"
- **Silent feminists** (**Kate Millett, Susan Brownmiller**) attacked "man the oppressor"

## Boomers in the Awakening

- Only 1 in 25 male Boomers saw combat; 10x as many committed draft felonies
- **Kent State (May 1970)** catalyzed national student strikes; 1972: half of eligible Boomers failed to vote
- After 1973 recession, Boomers found new reasons money-making was beneath them
- **Human Potential movement**, "Jesus freaks," born-agains—salvation no longer required lifetime of G.I. works
- Like **Hillary Clinton** at Wellesley: "choosing a way to live that will demonstrate the way we feel"
- **Todd Gitlin**: "time to go straight... from marijuana to white wine"

## 13ers Entering Childhood: Rosemary's Baby

- **1964**: fertility rate entered steep decline; by mid-1965, third of mothers admitted having unwanted children
- ***Rosemary's Baby*** (1968) anchored 20-year period of bad-kid movies; G-rated films fell from 41% to 13%
- **Disney laid off cartoonists for only time in history**
- **"Cult of the child"** replaced by **"cult of the adult"** (**Leslie Fiedler** / **Landon Jones**)
- **Abortion rate**: by late 1970s, one fetus in three aborted
- Awakening-era parents spent **40% less time on child raising** than parents in the High
- Divorce struck 13ers harder than any child generation; by **1980, just 56%** lived with two once-married parents
- Nurture shifted from authoritative to permissive; **Neil Postman**: children given "answers to questions they never asked"
- **Open Education**: replace facts/rules/grades with "tools and clay and freedom"; SAT scores plummeted
- Mid-1970s: **child poverty** passed directly from elder Lost to child 13th, skipping three generations; child suicide rate broke records

## Toward the Third Turning

- By early 1980s, anti-institutional doubts from Haight-Ashbury now heard in Wall Street chatter
- **Roland Stromberg** predicted: eventually mood "will be revealed as simply one more exhausted option"
- **The Second Turning necessarily had to give way to Third**

## Chapter 8: The Third Turning — Culture Wars (1984-2005?)

- **Reagan's 1984**: "Morning in America"—symbolized defeat of Establishment, primacy of the self
- **Apple's Super Bowl ad**: sledgehammer to Big Brother—"1984 won't be like *1984*"
- **Barbara Whitehead**: America shifted from "economy of abundance and psychology of scarcity" to the reverse
- **Senator Moynihan**: "deviancy kept getting redefined downward"
- **Culture Wars**: multiculturalists vs. traditionalists, secularists vs. evangelicals, planners vs. libertarians
- **Irving Kristol**: "profound division over what kind of country we are"
- 1994 election: voters split more ideologically than ever in history of polling
- **Clinton** proclaimed "the era of big government is over" while presiding over big government
- By mid-1990s, **3 in 4 adults** believed U.S. in "moral and spiritual decline"

### 1984 Threshold Trends
- Large deficits, falling savings, heavy foreign borrowing became political way of life
- **Mario Cuomo's "Two Americas"** and **Charles Murray's *Losing Ground***: widening rich-poor gap
- Married couples with children shrank to **26% of households** (from 40% in 1970)
- Child death by gunfire **tripled**; typical child saw 10,000 TV acts of mayhem by age 18
- Gambling exploded: more Americans visited casinos than attended MLB, NFL, symphonies combined

### Seven Historical Third Turnings
- *Retreat from France* (1435-1459), *Intolerance and Martyrdom* (1542-1569), *Reaction and Restoration* (1649-1675), *French and Indian Wars* (1746-1773), *Mexican War and Sectionalism* (1844-1860), *World War I and Prohibition* (1908-1929), *Culture Wars* (1984-2005?)

### Common Unraveling Patterns
- New social priority: **atomize, not gather; harvesting, not sowing**
- **Equinox era**: demand and supply of social order both falling
- Pace of life quickens, time horizons shorten; mounting problems either deferred or deemed insoluble

## Silent Entering Elderhood

- **No other U.S. generation** entered old age addressing so many public wants with so few results
- America's wealthiest-ever elders; Silent CEO earns **225x average worker pay** (vs. G.I. CEO at 41x)
- **David Broder**: "Fractured Generation" with "flip-flopping inconsistency"
- Well on way to becoming **first generation never to produce a president**—preeminent **helpmate generation** instead
- Mid-1980s Silent Congress: 2x hearings, 4x staff, 6x constituent mail, **1/3 as many laws** as G.I.s
- Two decades of Silent plurality added **8x as much to national debt** as all earlier generations combined
- America became **Michael Sandel's "procedural republic"**
- **Colin Powell**: "Issues come and go. Process is always important"

## Boomers Entering Midlife: The Cultural Elite

- 1984 *New York Times* declared "Year of the Yuppie"—*transcendental acquisition*
- 1987 stock crash ended yuppiemania; talk of family values filled airwaves
- **Clinton**: smart but distractible, gifted at gestures of empathy; reminded people of generation's best and worst
- 1994: first election determined by Boomer votes; government went from "moon walk to gridlock to train wreck"
- Boomers busy **"respirtualizing American culture and resacralizing its institutions"**
- **Robert Putnam's** "bowling alone" even as they tried restoring foundation for public virtue
- **Newt Gingrich**: "values" as "a way of dividing America"
- Evangelical/New Age growth: 2 in 3 Boomers say touched by supernatural power
- **Promise Keepers**, **Million Man March**: men holding hands in football stadia
- **Bennett's *Book of Virtues***: Unraveling's best-selling children's book with eleven teachable virtues (not including individuality or creativity)

## 13ers Entering Young Adulthood

- ***Nation at Risk***: "rising tide of mediocrity"; **Diane Ravitch/Chester Finn** graded youths: 20 F's, 8 D's, 1 C-minus
- 13er arrest rate: **117 per 1,000** (vs. Silent: 13 per 1,000)
- **One-generation depression**: 1973-1992 real median income for young males fell **28%**
- **Modular people**: deal with each situation on own terms, unlike Boomers who link everything to core self
- Most incarcerated generation; roughly 1/3 of all 13er black males in prison/probation/supervision
- More 13ers believe in **UFOs than Social Security** lasting until retirement
- **Youngest-copulating, oldest-marrying generation** ever recorded
- By any measure **least racist generation**: black-white marriages quadrupled in one generation

### Into the Oh-Ohs
- **First generation born this century less certifiably professional than predecessors**
- By end of Unraveling, median age at first marriage expected to be lower
- Will become **intensely frugal, loyal to kin, faithful to spouses, protective of children**

## Millennials Entering Childhood

- **Cabbage Patch Kids** (1983) and **Baby on Board** signs heralded new generation
- Demon-child films flopped, replaced by wanted-baby stories (*Three Men and a Baby*, *Baby Boom*)
- **"Decade of the Child"**: V-chip, youth curfews in 3/4 of largest cities, deadbeat dad laws
- **David Blankenhorn's "new familism"**: reversing Awakening's rise in divorce/abortion
- **Bennett's *Book of Virtues***: medicinal cover, scant humor, no individuality or creativity among virtues
- School uniforms spread nationally; **Clinton** supported uniforms and curfews despite Awakening youth
- New educational buzzwords: **collaborative learning, core values, zero tolerance**
- From ***Sesame Street*** to ***Barney***: programming went urban→pastoral, kinetic→lyrical, self-discovery→sharing

## Toward the Fourth Turning

- Year 2001: same old Culture Wars, political gridlock; mood "a lot more jittery"
- **"Pearl Harbor syndrome"** talk becomes tired cliche; more people rooting for something big
- Institutions at maximum weakness, individualism at maximum strength
- People feel less anxiety over prospect of casualties; society **ready for dramatic event**

> "The Fourth Turning will be at hand."

## Chapter 9: Fourth Turnings in History

> "Something happened to America at that time... Let's call it the Spirit of America." —**Senator Daniel Inouye**, V-J Day 1995

- Spirit comes once a saeculum, only through **ekpyrosis**—nature's fiery moment of death and discontinuity
- **Fourth Turning as winter solstice**: supply of social order falling, demand rising
- Passes nadir early—like coldest days of winter being days of lengthening sun

### Four Phases of Crisis
1. **Catalyst**: startling event producing sudden mood shift
2. **Regeneracy**: new counterentropy reunifying civic life
3. **Climax**: crucial moment confirming death of old order, birth of new
4. **Resolution**: triumphant or tragic conclusion establishing new order

### What Makes Crisis Special
- Public willingness to let leaders lead even when they falter
- Even spectacular failure (Pearl Harbor) does not undermine support
- Private life transforms: family order strengthens, substance abuse declines
- **Winner-take-all gives way to social sharing**; questions settled on grounds of **survival, not fairness**

### Six Prior Fourth Turnings
1. **Wars of the Roses** (1459-1487)
2. **Armada Crisis** (1569-1594)
3. **Glorious Revolution** (1675-1704)
4. **American Revolution** (1773-1794)
5. **Civil War** (1860-1865)
6. **Great Depression and WWII** (1929-1946)

### Great Power Saeculum: Black Tuesday to Pearl Harbor
- **F. Scott Fitzgerald**: "The shows were broader, the buildings were higher, the morals were looser"
- **Black Tuesday, October 29, 1929**: mood shift was "bewilderingly rapid"
- **FDR's Hundred Days** (Spring 1933): regeneracy in full swing; "The only thing we have to fear is fear itself"

### Civil War: Compressed Timeline
- War arrived more abruptly than Americans might think; Lincoln's election was point of no return
- **Civil War anomaly**: Third and Fourth Turnings together covered span of just one generation, produced no Hero archetype
- Two lessons: (1) Fourth Turning admits acceleration; (2) acceleration can add to tragedy

### Core Pattern

> "In every prior Fourth Turning, the catalyst was foreseeable but the climax was not."

### The Single Fourth Turning Constellation
- Since dawn of modern world, only one: **elder Prophets, midlife Nomads, young-adult Heroes, child Artists**
- Recurring exactly the same way five times over half millennium
- **Aging of Prophet is critical**: Crisis catalyst occurs when Prophet reaches apex of societal leadership

## Chapter 10: A Fourth Turning Prophecy

- **Sometime around 2005**, America will enter Fourth Turning
- Spark could seem as ominous as financial crash, as ordinary as national election, as trivial as Tea Party

### Plausible Scenarios (circa 2005)
- **Fiscal crisis** with state secession, tax rebellion, Constitutional Convention demands
- **Global terrorist group** with portable nuclear weapons; preemptive strikes; Congress declares war
- **Federal budget impasse** triggering government shutdown, default, Wall Street panic
- **Communicable virus** with mandatory quarantine, National Guard cordons, martial law
- **Former Soviet republic anarchy** prompting Russian military exercises, U.S. troop deployment

### The Great Devaluation
- **Implosion of societal trust** striking financial markets and economy
- People realize they've grown **helplessly dependent on teetering edifice of anonymous transactions and paper guarantees**
- Could result in severe drop in market price of most financial and real assets
- Economy may reach trough looking like start of depression; from this, **new social contract arises**

### Political and Social Transformation
- **Sweeping political realignment** ending era of split government
- America becomes less globally dependent, businesses more unionized, savings rate higher
- **Institutions increasingly bossy**, limiting personal freedoms, cleansing culture
- Criminal justice swift and rough; vagrants rounded up, executions hastened
- **All of America's lesser problems combine into one giant problem**

> "The Fourth Turning will be a time of glory or ruin."

## Boomers Entering Elderhood: Gray Champions

- **Missionary Generation** precedent: transformed elderhood from friendly to stern; FDR was leader "young men followed as they had followed no American since Lincoln"
- **Craig Karpel**: Boomers' elderhood like journey to "Owl Mountain, a primordial sanctuary"
- "Conscious aging" movement: elders as "wisdom keepers" with "dormant powers of intuition"
- Will resist aging, then glorify it—establish elegant insignia of advanced age
- Word *retirement* will acquire negative meaning; goal: to *replenish* or *reflect* or *pray*
- **Great Devaluation** likely to hit Boomers at vortex of downward spiral; savings could vanish in days
- Will construct new social ethic of decline/death—explore **thanatos** as they once probed **eros**

> The Gray Champion will appear as "figure of an ancient man... combining the leader and the saint"

- Same Boomers who chanted "Hell no, we won't go!" emerge as **America's most martial elder generation**
- Will risk enormous pain to command youth to fight/die in ways they never would have tolerated in own youth

## 13ers Entering Midlife: Doom Players

- **Lost Generation** precedent: **F. Scott Fitzgerald** called them "generation with no second acts"—they proved him wrong
- **Ernest Hemingway**: "What is moral is what you feel good after"
- 13er pop culture archetype: young protagonist alone, unprepared, chosen by chance to decide fate of humanity—zeroes in, does what's required, comes out on top

### Crisis Role
- Will feel little stake in old order; never known time when America felt good about itself
- **"We're not worthy"** streak of weak collective esteem will render claims unusually selfless
- Will vote against own short-term interests if persuaded community's survival requires it
- Strategically located between moralistic Boomers and cherished Millennials
- **Only ones capable of deflecting more dangerous Boomer tendencies**
- A 13er may be the intrepid statesman preventing some righteous old Aquarian from **loosing fateful lightning**

### Potential for Extremism
- Charismatic anti-intellectual demagogue could convert ad slogans into political slogans: "No excuses." "Just do it."
- At most extreme, product could be **new American fascism**

## Millennials Entering Young Adulthood: Power Rangers

- **G.I. precedent**: 100,000 chanted in 1933: "I will help President Roosevelt bring back good times"
- Youth took **most dramatically positive change ever recorded** just before Crash of 1929
- **FDR**: "America cannot always build the future for our youth, but we can build our youth for the future"
- **Power Rangers**: wholesome kid-soldiers in primary-color uniforms; motto: **The Power of Teamwork Overcomes All**
- Pop culture becomes **"big, bland, and friendly"**; first Millennial celebrities praised as good role models
- Will delight employers with skills, work habits, institutional loyalties
- Will revitalize trade unions, treat coworkers as partners
- If inducted for war, cast aside pacifism and march to duty—**not averse to militarized mass violence, just oppose uncontrolled personal violence**

> "Near the climax of Crisis, the full power of this rising generation will assert itself."

## New Silent Entering Childhood

- **Silent Depression-era precedent**: adults ruled with stern hand; **Literary Digest** demanded "reassertion of parental authority"
- Crisis-era children: vulnerable seeds of society's future, tethered close to home doing helpful deeds
- What felt like sensible protections in Unraveling reach **stifling suffocation** in Crisis
- **Least immigrant, most English-speaking generation** in living memory
- **13ers as dominant nurturers** look back on own chaotic childhoods, pride in firmer family life—but New Silent will eventually recall it as smothering overcorrection

- **Revolution**: commerce vs. citizenship

## How America Should Prepare

### Prepare Values
- Culture Warriors need not worry whether values return—they always do in Fourth Turning
- Cultivate pragmatic alliances; **decadent or nihilistic culture is seedbed for fascism**

### Prepare Institutions
- Shed and simplify federal government; turn local governments into competitive policy labs
- Courts should avoid erecting constitutional obstacles to legislative action

### Prepare Politics
- Speak candidly about future challenges; craft rhetoric of public purpose emphasizing duties over rights
- Accept that fundamental reforms not now possible in Unraveling

### Prepare Society
- Battle civic dysfunction; experiment with **compulsory youth service**
- Communities improve own functions with own resources

### Prepare Youth
- **Millennial Generation is Unraveling-era America's most important investment priority**
- Shift benefit budget from nonneedy old toward needy young

### Prepare Elders
- Save much more; shift FICA to fully funded plans; brace for self-reliant old age

### Prepare the Economy
- Raise national savings; shift taxes from income toward consumption
- Aim for budget surplus; risk Third Turning recession to mitigate Fourth Turning depression

### Prepare the Defense
- Gird for war whose scale, cost, casualties far exceed anything tolerable now
- Don't overspecialize or overinvest in deployments rendered obsolete when Crisis climaxes

## Personal Preparation

- **The Blizzard Metaphor**: picture yourself in howling blizzard lasting several years—that is how to plan for saecular winter
- **Rectify**: build reputation as person of honor; classic virtues become hard currency
- **Converge**: team, brand, standard become new catchwords; appearances matter
- **Bond**: face-to-face contacts newly important; expect loss of personal privacy
- **Gather**: raise children to excel at team virtues; expect compulsory service
- **Root**: family becomes ultimate safety net; multigenerational households function best
- **Brace**: discount government promises about Social Security/Medicare reliability
- **Hedge**: diversify everything; avoid leveraged investments; enter Crisis with reliable cash flow

## Generational Scripts

### G.I. Script
- Must accept that Millennial well-being (not their own) must be government's highest priority
- By relinquishing unneeded portion of reward, transform from senior citizens to **citizen seniors**

### Silent Script
- Must avoid intruding on moral judgments; avoid using complexity to stifle civic authority
- Kindest thing for Millennials: **be the helpmate**

### Boomer Script
- Must defuse Culture Wars, unite around national survival
- Display **forbearance** never associated with them; if allow self-indulgence to overflow into public life, will **bankrupt children financially, themselves morally**
- **Franklin's** answer for national seal: **Moses, parting the waters for his people**

### 13er Script
- **Survival skills** are exactly what society needs; must gather them for one larger purpose
- Must keep Boomers from wreaking needless destruction and Millennials from marching too mindlessly
- If play script weakly, **old Boomers could wreak horrible apocalypse**; if cleverly, **a new golden age**

> "Old men do not grow wise. They grow careful." —**Hemingway**

### Millennial Script
- Whether Crisis won or lost depends in large measure on Hero's **teamwork, competence, and courage**
- Should heed traditional values lessons despite hypocrisy of those preaching them

## Chapter 12: The Eternal Return

- **Navajo sand circles**: four seasons drawn counterclockwise, small gap left for **ekpyrosis**—moment of death and rebirth only God can close
- **Nietzsche's eternal return**: every event perpetually reenacted, every act an end in itself
- **Saeculum provides this same opportunity**: every person living to deep old age experiences each of four turnings once

### Five Possible Outcomes
1. **End of man** (omnicide)—possible but not likely
2. **End of modernity**—New Dark Ages
3. **End of America as nation**—each prior Crisis produced moments of extreme danger
4. **End of Millennial Saeculum**—America reborn, for better or worse
5. **Magnificent First Turning**—the more magnificent the High, the more powerful resulting generational tectonics

### Memory Spans
- Authors' spans: **1881 to 2104** (Strauss) and **1888 to 2114** (Howe)—longer than America has existed
- A girl born today could witness another Crisis catalyze on eve of twenty-second century

> "History is seasonal, but its outcomes are not foreordained."

### Cycle-Theory Predecessors
- **Arnold Toynbee, Quincy Wright, Giuseppe Ferrari, Samuel Huntington, Frank Klingberg**, the two **Arthur Schlesingers**

### The Authors' Collaboration
- Interest originated in Strauss's books on Vietnam-era draft and Howe's on long-term fiscal problems
- Presumed generational dynamics were unlike anything before—but **only the details were new; the underlying rhythms were not**

> *To every thing there is a season, and a time to every purpose under heaven.* —**Ecclesiastes 3.1-8**
