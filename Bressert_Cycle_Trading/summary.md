# Walter Bressert — Cycle Trading Collection
## Summary

Three works combined into one shelf entry:
1. **The Power of Oscillator/Cycle Combinations** (1991, 218 pages) — the core textbook
2. **Cycles, Patterns and Oscillators in the Stock Market** (1992 TAG XIV Workshop, 47 pages) — condensed workshop applying the methodology to stocks
3. **The Cycle Trading Pattern Manual** (1997-1998, 24 pages) — streamlined reference for the Setup/Trigger entry system with Cycle Trading Patterns

---

## How the Three Works Fit Together

The **book** is the foundation. It lays out the full cycle hierarchy, teaches centered detrending to find cycles, introduces every oscillator Bressert uses, and walks through detailed research tables for gold, T-bonds, soybeans, soybean oil, Japanese yen, and the S&P. Chapters build sequentially from cycle theory through oscillator techniques to money management.

The **workshop** is Bressert distilling the book into a live presentation for stock market traders. It adds the 4-Year Cycle in the DJIA with historical tables going back to 1917, presidential election year analysis, and the Bear Kiss OSCAR pattern. It demonstrates the same oscillator/cycle methodology applied to the DJIA, S&P, currencies, and T-bonds in real time (1992).

The **manual** is the latest evolution — a concise, pattern-focused guide that drops the historical research detail and focuses on how to actually build and trade Cycle Trading Patterns. It introduces the EMA% Trend, MACD Trend Indicator, Keltner Channel, and trendline confirmation as pattern components. It is the most practical of the three.

## Core Philosophy

- There is no magic oscillator. Knowledge, self-discipline, and persistence are the keys.
- Fundamentals move markets, but the fundamental picture is always most bullish at tops and most bearish at bottoms. Cycle analysis gives confidence to trade against prevailing sentiment.
- Cycles provide TIME. Oscillators provide PRICE confirmation. Together they form Oscillator/Cycle Combinations (OSCARs) that mechanically identify cycle tops and bottoms.
- Stand-alone buy/sell signals do not consider the whole picture. Combining signals with cycles, retracements, trend indicators, and trendlines into Cycle Trading Patterns greatly improves accuracy.

## The Cycle Hierarchy

From longest to shortest, every market contains nested cycles:

| Level | Typical Length | Measurement |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| **Long-Term** | 2-11 years (varies by market) | Monthly charts |
| **Seasonal** | ~1 year | Weekly charts |
| **Primary Cycle (PC)** | 9-31 weeks (varies by market) | Weekly charts |
| **1/2 Primary Cycle** | ~Half the PC | Weekly/daily |
| **Trading Cycle (TC)** | 14-25 bars avg (typically ~20) | Daily charts |
| **Alpha Cycle** | First half of TC | Daily/intraday |
| **Beta Cycle** | Second half of TC | Daily/intraday |

Each longer cycle determines the TREND for the next shorter cycle. If the longer cycle is moving up, buy the troughs of the shorter cycle. If the longer cycle is moving down, sell the crests of the shorter cycle.

### Key Market Cycle Lengths

| Market | Primary Cycle | Trading Cycle |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| S&P / DJIA | 22 weeks | 39 market days |
| T-Bonds | 21 weeks (also 14-week) | 21 market days |
| Gold | 18 weeks | 15 market days |
| Japanese Yen | 31 weeks (26-44 TB) | 8 weeks |
| Soybeans | 14 weeks | 20 market days |

### Right and Left Translation

- **Right Translation (Bull Market):** Cycle crest leans right. More time spent rising, less declining. Higher crests and higher troughs. TC tends to crest with the Beta Cycle crest.
- **Left Translation (Bear Market):** Cycle crest leans left. More time spent declining, less rising. Lower troughs and lower crests. TC tends to crest with the Alpha Cycle crest.

Translation direction tells you the trend of the next larger cycle.

## 70% Timing Bands

Timing bands are ranges within which 70% of cycle tops/bottoms have occurred historically. Distribution:
- 20% occur BEFORE the band
- 70% occur WITHIN the band
- 10% occur AFTER the band

By the last day of the timing band, 90%+ of cycles would have already topped or bottomed. The probability of a successful trade is much greater later in the timing band than earlier.

### Seven Measurable Cyclic Components
Each Trading Cycle (or Primary Cycle) has 7 measurable timing components:
1. Trading Cycle Trough-to-Trough
2. Alpha Cycle Trough-to-Trough
3. Beta Cycle Trough-to-Trough
4. Alpha Cycle Trough-to-Crest
5. Alpha Cycle Crest-to-Trough
6. Beta Cycle Trough-to-Crest
7. Beta Cycle Crest-to-Trough

The overlap area of Bands 1, 5, and 7 gives the most probable time for the Trading Cycle trough.

### Bull vs. Bear Timing Band Adjustment
- **Bull market:** T-C band shifts later (average to END); C-T band shifts earlier (BEGINNING to average)
- **Bear market:** T-C band shifts earlier (BEGINNING to average); C-T band shifts later (average to END)

## The Oscillator/Cycle Combination Framework

### Seven Techniques to Enhance Oscillator Performance
1. **Price/Oscillator Turns** — simplest; oscillator and price turn together
2. **Smoothing** — MA of the oscillator eliminates false wiggles (e.g., RSI3M3 = RSI(3) smoothed with 3-bar MA)
3. **Levels (Buy/Sell Lines)** — identify extremes where tops/bottoms form
4. **Crossover Lines** — slower confirmation filter (e.g., 5-term MA of the oscillator)
5. **Zero Line** — support/resistance for oscillators; key component in patterns
6. **Price/Oscillator Patterns** — combine multiple techniques into recurring patterns
7. **Setup/Trigger Entry** — turn patterns into mechanical trades

### The Setup/Trigger Entry System

The core of Bressert's approach. Every trade follows a mechanical two-step process:

**SETUP:** An oscillator formation that can incorporate any combination of the seven techniques — oscillator drops below Buy Line, turns up, crosses above crossover, diverges with price, forms a Kiss or Hook pattern, etc.

**TRIGGER ENTRY:** Price activity makes the final entry decision. Usually:
- **Buy:** A buy stop above the high of the setup bar (the day/week that turned the oscillator up)
- **Sell:** A sell stop below the low of the setup bar (the day/week that turned the oscillator down)

Once entered, a protective stop goes below the cycle low (for longs) or above the cycle high (for shorts).

### The Three-Step Development Process
1. TIME is right for a cycle high or low (prices within timing band)
2. The Price/Oscillator Pattern sets up
3. Price exceeds the Trigger entry stop to put you in the market

## The Oscillators

### RSI3M3 (Primary Short-Term Oscillator)
- **Construction:** Regular RSI with period=3, smoothed with a 3-bar moving average
- **Buy Line:** 30 (daily T-bonds), 55 (weekly DJIA)
- **Sell Line:** 60-70 (varies by market)
- **Crossover:** 5-bar MA of the RSI3M3
- **RSI3M3 Detrend:** RSI3M3 minus its 5-bar crossover — catches trending market bottoms that the RSI3M3 misses
- **Combined accuracy:** RSI3M3 + RSI3M3 Detrend together identified ALL seven trading cycle bottoms and six of seven half-cycle bottoms in the T-bond example
- **Mechanical buy signal:** RSI3M3 drops below 30, turns up (setup bar), buy stop one tick above high of setup bar, protective stop below cycle low

### CCI (Commodity Channel Index)
- **Standard levels:** Buy Line at -100, Sell Line at +100
- **Smoothed CCI (CCI58):** 5-day CCI smoothed with 8-term MA; Buy Line at -35, Sell Line at +60
- **CCI with Crossover (cxo8):** 8-term MA crossover of the CCI
- **CCI30 (Monthly):** 30-period CCI with 8-month crossover — confirmed every S&P 4-Year Cycle bottom
- **4-Week T-Bond TC pattern:** CCI drops below -35 from above 60, turns up → buy stop above high of upturn day

### MACD Detrend
- **Construction:** Standard MACD (26, 12, 9 EMA). Detrend = MACD minus its Signal line. Then smooth with 3-term MA as crossover.
- **Key advantage:** Detrend turns BEFORE the MACD itself, often at the exact week of tops/bottoms
- **Gold Primary Cycle:** Three patterns identified 80% of PC lows — Oversold, Kiss, and Hook
- **Japanese Yen:** Detrend high matched the price high week in 3 of 3 major tops; MACD lagged by 2+ weeks

### 3-10 Oscillator
- **Construction:** 3-day MA minus 10-day MA
- **Crossover:** 16-term MA of the 3-10 oscillator
- **More sensitive than MACD** — identifies shorter-term moves
- **Two key sell patterns:**
  - **Two Step Sell (TSS):** High A above crossover → low B below crossover → higher high C above A = "second step" → 2-day downturn = setup
  - **Small Bump Sell (SBS):** Low A below crossover → high B above crossover/zero → lower low C below A → high D above crossover but below B = "small bump" → 2-day downturn = setup
- **Gold result:** Every Primary Cycle high was followed by a completed TSS or SBS sell pattern; 4 of 5 PC highs entered within 7 days of the top
- **Entry rules:** 8-Week Rule (no outside down week at 8-week high), 6-Week Rule (if <6 weeks from PC low, cancel after 3 weeks), 14-Week Rule (don't enter after week 14 from PC low)

### Stochastic
- **Use multiple time frames:** 10-day (Trading Cycle), 20-day (1/2 Primary), 40-day (Primary)
- **Adjusted levels:** Buy at 20 (not 30), Sell at 80 (not 70) for 10-week
- **T-Bond PC bottoms:** 15 of 18 occurred with 10-week Stochastic below 20; 80% dropped without a wiggle from sell to buy territory
- **Soybeans seasonal:** 20-week Stochastic — 6 of 8 seasonal highs above 80, every seasonal low below 15

### Detrend (Price Detrend as Oscillator)
- **Centered Detrend:** Best for historical cycle identification; lags by half the cycle (not real-time)
- **Half-Span Detrend:** MA at half the cycle length — approximates centered detrend in real-time (e.g., 10-week detrend for 20-week cycle)
- **Detrend Levels:** Empirically determined overbought/oversold levels that signal imminent tops/bottoms
- **Crossover Detrend:** Gives better buy patterns than standard oscillators in trending markets

## Key Oscillator Patterns

### The Kiss Pattern
- **Bull Kiss:** After rising from a low, detrend rises above zero, then drops back to test/kiss the crossover without breaking down, then turns up again → buy signal
- **Bear Kiss:** Mirror image — detrend drops below zero, rallies to test/kiss crossover, fails, turns down → sell signal
- **Reliability enhanced when:** Price low before the Kiss is above the previous low (bull) or price high is below the previous high (bear)

### The Hook Pattern (Gold MACD Detrend)
- Oscillator drops below -1, rises (generally below zero), turns down, drops below crossover, then rises back above crossover → buy signal
- All 4 Hook patterns rose 10%+ from PC low

### The Oversold Pattern (Gold MACD Detrend)
- Oscillator drops below -1 (range: -1 to -11), turns up, exceeds crossover → buy stop above crossover week high
- Most common in bear markets; average rise of 12.2%, PC high within 9 weeks

### Bear Kiss OSCAR (Daily S&P)
- 3-10 oscillator rises from below crossover for 2+ days, turns down while still below crossover (setup)
- Trigger: drop below low of downturn day
- Expect TC or Alpha Cycle bottom 2-8 days later; decline of 0.9-6.2%

## Cycle Trading Patterns (from the Manual)

The complete Cycle Trading Pattern combines multiple components:
1. **Trend direction confirmed** — weekly EMA trend up (fast EMA above slow EMA, both rising), weekly MACD above signal and rising
2. **Price overextension** — drop below faster EMA line while it is still rising; or drop below Keltner Channel midline but above lower channel
3. **Fibonacci retracement** — 38-62% retracement of the prior cycle move (5 of 6 TC bottoms retrace at least 38%; closing below 62% signals trend reversal)
4. **Timing band** — within the TC bottoming window
5. **Mechanical entry** — RSI3M3 setup and trigger buy signal

### Trend Indicators
- **EMA Trend:** Two weekly EMAs (fast/slow). Trend UP when fast above slow and moving up.
- **EMA% Trend:** Percentage difference between EMAs — downturn signals early trend reversal warning
- **MACD Trend:** When both MACD and EMA moving same direction, odds strongly favor trending move
- **Keltner Channel:** 5-week MA with 1.1 standard deviation bands. Drop below midline with price above lower band + RSI3M3 buy = strong pattern

### Trendline Confirmation
- Uptrend lines across TC bottoms; penetration confirms weekly cycle top
- Downtrend line penetration confirms weekly cycle bottom and signals a sizable upmove (21-week average cycle)
- Close-only trendline breaks are more significant than bar chart penetration

## 3-Dimensional Analysis

Evaluate markets on three levels simultaneously:
- **Monthly data:** Long-term cycles (>1 year), seasonal identification
- **Weekly data:** Seasonal and Primary Cycle highs/lows
- **Daily data:** Primary Cycle and Trading Cycle identification

When oscillators across multiple timeframes align at overbought/oversold extremes, the probability of a major top/bottom increases dramatically.

## Money Management: The 3-Contract System

### Contract No.1 — The Money Contract
- Profit objective equals the dollar risk (typically $1,000)
- Take profit as quickly as possible (within 5 days)
- Once liquidated, total risk drops by two-thirds and $1,000 is locked in

### Contract No.2 — Short-Term Profit
- Take profit at TC crest (bull) or TC trough (bear)
- After No.1 profit, raise stop to 1/2 point below entry — total exposure now $500 for 2 positions
- Exit on oscillator sell signal (CCI rise above sell line, downturn, drop below downturn day low)

### Contract No.3 — Long-Term Profit
- Ride to the Primary Cycle or Seasonal Cycle price objective
- After No.2 profit, raise stop to breakeven plus commissions — trade now guaranteed profitable
- Wide stop needed; often risks 20-30% of open profits
- Big moves (once every 2 years) more than compensate for extra No.3 losses

### Risk Rules
- Never risk more than 10% of equity on any single trade (5% is better)
- Never risk more than 20% in any one complex
- Reduce risk percentage as equity grows (not increase it)
- Pre-determine an equity level at which you remove profits from your account

### T-Bond Example Results (3-Contract System)
- 12 trades, 11 winners, 1 loser (91.7% win rate)
- Net profit: $58,880 averaging $5,350 per 3-contract trade
- No.1 profits: $10,000 total; No.2: $14,240; No.3: $38,960
- Dollar risk < $1,500/contract in 10 of 11 trades

## The 12 Cardinal Mistakes of Futures Trading

1. **Lack of a Game Plan** — 90%+ of traders have none
2. **Lack of Money Management** — probability of being right matters more than reward/risk ratio
3. **Failure to Use Protective Stops** — real stops, not mental stops. "The first loss is the smallest."
4. **Taking Small Profits, Letting Losses Run** — result of no game plan
5. **Overstaying Your Position** — not taking profits when objectives are met
6. **Averaging a Loss** — only acceptable if pre-determined in the game plan with unmovable stops
7. **Meeting Margin Calls** — means you are wrong; close the position
8. **Increasing Commitment with Success** — most dangerous mistake; largest losses come from overconfidence
9. **Overtrading Your Account** — never risk more than 5-10% per trade regardless of conviction
10. **Failure to Remove Profits** — pre-determine equity levels to withdraw; equity moves in cycles too
11. **Changing Strategy During Market Hours** — fear and greed make you irrational; do homework when markets are closed
12. **Lack of Patience / Trading for Excitement** — average futures trader lifespan: 5 minutes to 9 months

### Trading Psychology
- You are most likely to make these mistakes when MAKING money, not losing it
- Following success comes overconfidence, then sloppy discipline, then large losses
- Hope is the most devastating emotion — when you find yourself hoping, get out immediately
- Self-discipline is the single greatest determinant of success

## Research Methodology

Bressert's approach to developing any Oscillator/Cycle Combination:
1. Research minimum 5 years (preferably 10-20) of historical data
2. Learn one oscillator well before moving to others
3. Do not rely on a single oscillator — use combinations
4. Keep detailed research notebooks; review periodically for patterns
5. Catalog patterns by type, sort by multiple columns to find hidden timing
6. Review Buy/Sell Lines every 6-12 months
7. Research over bull AND bear market conditions
8. Paper trade before committing capital

### Price Tools Used with Cycles
- **Mid-Cycle Pause (MCP):** Price objective for next cycle crest/trough based on diagonal distance and vertical projection
- **60-40% Retracements:** Fibonacci .618/.382 zones for cycle lows (bull) and highs (bear); only valid if next longer cycle continues in same direction
- **Speed Resistance Lines:** 1/3 and 2/3 divisions of cycle range from Edson Gould; penetration of upper line drives to lower, penetration of lower tests the cycle low
- **Support/Resistance Lines:** Horizontal lines from MCP diagonal midpoints; can hold for months or years

## The 4-Year Cycle in the Stock Market (Workshop)

- Traceable back to 1789 in the U.S. stock market
- Average 49 months low-to-low, 36 months low-to-high, 13 months high-to-low
- Average advance: 107%
- When the 4-Year Cycle exceeds the previous cycle high: average 127% advance, 45 months to top, only 6 months high-to-low
- When it does NOT exceed the previous high: average 96% advance, only 24 months to top, 20 months high-to-low
- CCI30 on monthly data confirmed every 4-Year Cycle bottom (5 requirements: 32+ months from previous bottom, detrend below -90 and turns up, CCI below zero, CCI rises above crossover, prices exceed crossover month high)

## Key Trading Rules

1. Trade with the trend — if weekly cycle is up, buy daily dips; if down, sell rallies
2. Do not buy in a downtrending market (6 of 8 buy signals in downtrend were losers)
3. Expect TC bottoms to retrace 38-62% of the prior move; a close below 62% signals trend reversal
4. The probability of making a successful trade is much greater later in the timing band than earlier
5. No single oscillator picks every top and bottom — use several in combination
6. Research any oscillator pattern over minimum 5 years before trading it
7. The first loss is the smallest and easiest to take
8. When in doubt, stay out; if in doubt, get out
9. Following several profitable trades, reduce risk percentage rather than increase it
10. Remove profits from your trading account at pre-determined equity levels
