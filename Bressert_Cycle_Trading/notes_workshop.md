## SECTION 1: CYCLES (pp.2-17)

### The Natural Order of Markets (p.2)

- The order of the Universe shows itself in thousands of observable cycles documented by the Foundation for the Study of Cycles
- Cycles visible from minute structures of molecules/atoms/sub-atomic particles to ordered structure of solar system to largest clusters of galaxies
- Prices appear random but with study show underlying order: cycles, waves, Gann squares/angles, Fibonacci relationships in time and price, planetary influences
- "We do not know its causes, nor do we have a solid grasp of the rules... but anyone who studies the markets with an open mind will see that there is indeed an order to all markets"
- Energy of markets visible in repeating patterns of time and price in cycles and Elliott Waves
- Market oscillators reflect this energy as overbought and oversold levels
- Overbought/oversold levels are often cycle highs and lows
- By identifying dominant cycles, can anticipate tops/bottoms AND direction of trend/longer cycle
- Much explained in his book "The Power of Oscillator/Cycle Combinations"

- It is THE DOMINANT longer-term cycle affecting the stock market

### Time and Price Expectations for Next 4-Year Cycle High (p.3)

- Smallest advance of cycles in Table 2 was the 38% rise from 1978 low (occurred in broad trading range)
- Of cycles not in trading range, smallest advance was 73%
- Of 11 cycles that exceeded previous cycle high: one rose minimum 38% (= DJIA 3260 from Oct '90 low of 2365); 10 of 11 (90%) advanced 67-86% or more above cycle low (= price objective of 3950 to 4375)
- Cycles exceeding prev. high averaged 43 months low-to-high; those not exceeding averaged only 26 months
- Three cycles rose for 31-33 months; rest topped 42-60 months after cycle began

**Projection (as of 1992):**
- From October 1990 cycle bottom: top no earlier than April 1993 (31 months) to June (33 months)
- Continued rise after June 1993 likely, followed by rise to at least March 1994 (42 months) and possibly as late as November 1994 (50 months)
- Time window for 4-Year Cycle top: April 1993 through November 1994
- Price window: DJIA 3950 to 4375

### WILL THE FUNDAMENTALS SUPPORT THIS PROJECTION? (p.4)

- At time of writing, focus on national debt, bank failures, real estate problems made projection seem unrealistic
- BUT: looking at front page of Wall Street Journal following cycle bottoms in '74, '78, '82, '87 reveals similar doom and gloom — yet stock market was rising, and NO predictions of actual tops occurred at actual tops
- "Fundamentals of supply and demand move the markets, but the focus of the fundamental news is always the most bullish at tops and the most bearish at bottoms"
- Cycle analysis gives confidence to sell tops and buy bottoms with expectation that fundamental picture WILL change to move prices in direction of cycle
- Long-term trend is UP; still >2 years before 4-Year Cycle due to top

### THE WEEKLY PRIMARY CYCLE (pp.5-6)

- All markets have a powerful and consistent weekly cycle that sets the intermediate-term trend
- DJIA and S&P Index show a cycle of approximately 20 weeks measured low-to-low
- Cycles do NOT move in sine waves: they extend, contract, sometimes skip a beat
- Markets act differently in bull and bear markets:

**Right Translation (Bull Market):** cycle high leans to the RIGHT — more time spent rising, less declining
**Left Translation (Bear Market):** cycle high leans to the LEFT — more time spent declining

#### TIMING BANDS FOR BULL MARKET (80% accuracy, p.5-6):
- **Low to High:** expect cycle to top 9 to 16 weeks from cycle bottom
- **High to Low:** once topped, expect bottom 1 to 9 weeks from that top
- **Low to Low:** next low should occur 13 to 20 weeks from previous low
- Most probable time for bottom: overlap area of high-to-low and low-to-low timing bands
- These bands are 80% accurate; about 20% of tops/bottoms occur outside the bands

### BUY/SELL LINES (p.7)

- Both oscillators have Buy and Sell Lines as filters
- General guideline: oscillator rises above Sell Line and turns down at/before cycle top; drops below Buy Line and turns up at/before cycle bottom
- Often TWO oscillator highs in each 20-Week Cycle; the second usually occurs at cycle top
- Bull market right translation: cycle tops with the first oscillator high
- Bear market left translation: cycle tops with the first oscillator high

### FOUR STEPS TO IDENTIFY CYCLE TOPS AND BOTTOMS (p.7)

1. **Prices in timing window:** should be within the Time and Price Window, or at least within Timing Bands — eliminates most early turns in oscillators
2. **Oscillator at extreme:** one or both oscillators should have risen above Sell Line (for top) or dropped below Buy Line (for bottom)
3. **Oscillator turns:** oscillator should turn down at cycle top or turn up at bottom
4. **Mechanical Trigger Entry:** the real key — sell stop below price low of downturn week (for short), or buy stop above price high of upturn week (for long)

**Complementary Oscillators:**
- At Highs A and B (Chart 5): CCI 58 clearly identifies the tops
- At Lows C and D: RSI 33 better identifies the cycle bottoms
- These are Oscillator/Cycle Combinations (OSCARs) — can be historically tested, cataloged, and used to trade

### RIGHT AND LEFT TRANSLATION (p.11)

#### Chart 9: Three-Part Diagram

**Figure 3: A Simple Cycle** — symmetric zigzag sine wave

**Figure 4: The Trend of the Larger Cycle** — shows how a larger cycle (shown as dome/arc) modifies the simple cycle:
- When larger cycle trend is UP: smaller cycle crests lean to the right (peak occurs late in cycle)
- When larger cycle trend is DOWN: smaller cycle crests lean to the left (peak occurs early in cycle)

**Figure 5: Simple Cycle Combined with Trend**
- **Right Translation (Bull Market):** as trend of larger cycle moves UP, cycle peaks shift right — more time spent rising, shorter declines, cycle lows trend upward
- **Left Translation (Bear Market):** as trend of larger cycle moves DOWN, cycle peaks shift left — less time spent rising, longer declines, cycle lows trend downward

**Trading Implication:** direction of translation tells you the trend of the next larger cycle

#### Chart 12: Three Measurable Components of a Cycle

For a single Primary Cycle:
1. **Trough to Trough (T-T)** — full cycle length
2. **Trough to Crest (T-C)** — rising phase
3. **Crest to Trough (C-T)** — declining phase

For a Primary Cycle subdivided into two 1/2 Primary Cycles:
1. PC T-T (full Primary Cycle, trough to trough)
2. First 1/2 PC T-T
3. First 1/2 PC T-C
4. First 1/2 PC C-T
5. Second 1/2 PC T-T
6. Second 1/2 PC T-C
7. Second 1/2 PC C-T

= **7 measurable timing components** that can be quantified into timing bands

### THE SEVEN CYCLIC COMPONENTS OF THE TRADING CYCLE (p.15)

#### Chart 13: Seven Components of the Trading Cycle

The interplay between the Trading Cycle and Alpha/Beta Cycles has 7 cyclic components that project time periods for crests and troughs:

1. **Trading Cycle Trough to Trough** — full Trading Cycle
2. **Alpha Cycle Trough to Trough** — first half of Trading Cycle
3. **Beta Cycle Trough to Trough** — second half of Trading Cycle
4. **Alpha Cycle Trough to Crest** — rising phase of Alpha
5. **Alpha Cycle Crest to Trough** — declining phase of Alpha
6. **Beta Cycle Trough to Crest** — rising phase of Beta
7. **Beta Cycle Crest to Trough** — declining phase of Beta

Most markets follow this Trading Cycle / Alpha Cycle / Beta Cycle pattern.

### TIMING BAND PROBABILITY (p.16)

#### Chart 14: "The Probability of Making a Successful Trade is Much Greater Later in the Timing Bands Than Earlier"

- Timing Bands are ranges within which prices have topped/bottomed >7 out of 10 times historically
- Probability of success is MUCH GREATER later in the Timing Band than earlier

**70% Timing Band Distribution:**
- **20%** of cycle tops/bottoms occur BEFORE the Timing Band
- **70%** occur WITHIN the Timing Band
- **10%** occur AFTER the Timing Band
- By the last day of the 70% Timing Band: 90% or more of cycles would have already topped or bottomed

### TIME CYCLES/OSCILLATORS TO IDENTIFY TOPS & BOTTOMS (p.17)

- Using oscillators within cycle timing band periods greatly improves accuracy of identifying cycle tops/bottoms
- All markets have cycles but they contract, extend, or skip a beat
- Predictability greatly improved by using cycle time periods or timing bands measured from historical highs and lows

#### S&P Index Weekly Cycle Timing Bands (10 years of data, 80% accuracy):
- **80% of BOTTOMS** occurred in timing band of **13 to 20 weeks** from previous cycle bottom
- **80% of TOPS** occurred **9 to 16 weeks** from the low that began the cycle
- **80% of BOTTOMS** occurred **1 to 9 weeks** from the top

**Diagram (p.17):**
```
         9 ---------- 16 weeks low to high
        /\
       /  \  1 -------- 9 weeks high to low
      /    \
     /      \
    13 -------------- 20 weeks low to low
```

- Oscillators indicate overbought/oversold levels
- Cycle tops/bottoms are BY THEIR NATURE overbought/oversold levels tied to specific time periods
- By combining oscillators and cycles, tops and bottoms can often be identified within 1 to 3 bars of a top or bottom

## SECTION 5: CYCLEWATCH RESEARCH REPORT — BEAR KISS OSCAR (pp.39-40)

### BEAR KISS IN THE DAILY S&P FUTURES (p.39)

**Source:** Walter Bressert & Associates, 6987 N. Oracle, Tucson AZ 85704 (602/544-0404, 800/677-0120, FAX 602/544-0081)

**Data Note:** Futures data uses a "rollover" with past history adjusted for the spread between old and new contract on the last day of the month preceding delivery. This makes oscillators more consistent but somewhat distorts prices prior to current contract.

**The Bear Kiss OSCAR:**
- Occurs in the later stages of a downmove into a cycle bottom of the Trading Cycle or Alpha Cycle
- Can be used as a signal to go SHORT, as an indicator of a BOTTOM, and a BUY signal — or both

**Bear Kiss Formation Rules:**

1. The daily 3-10 Oscillator in the S&P futures rises from below the Crossover for two or more days and turns down while STILL below the Crossover. This downturn is the SETUP. Only the first downturn can generate an OSCAR — the 3-10 must rise above the Crossover and drop back below it to set up another OSCAR.

2. The Trigger Entry occurs with a drop in the S&P below the downturn day, which usually occurs within two days of the downturn day.

**Visual Diagram (p.39):**
```
SETUP                     SETUP
  ENTRY                     ENTRY
   ↓                          ↓
3-10 rises from below      3-10 rises from below
Crossover, turns down      Crossover, turns down
WHILE STILL BELOW          WHILE STILL BELOW
Crossover                  Crossover
```

#### Chart 46: S&P Daily (S&P-D) with Bear Kiss Pattern
- Upper panel: S&P daily from ~320 to ~389 with cycle lows labeled: AC, PC, AC, TC PC, AC PC, TC
- Lower panel: 3-10 Oscillator ranging from -10 to +10
- Arrows in lower panel point to the 3-10 oscillator high and downturn that set up the OSCAR
- Cycle labels: AC = Alpha Cycle low; TC = Trading Cycle low; PC = 22-Week Primary Cycle low; 1/2PC = 10-Week Half Primary Cycle low
- **Key observation:** 3 OSCARs occur in 4 months in the BEAR MARKET into the Sept/Oct 1990 low, and only 2 OSCARs occur in the BULL MARKET
- More frequent Bear Kiss signals during downtrends — useful for short-side trading

### CCI 30 OSC/CYCLE COMBO FOR 4-YEAR CYCLE BOTTOMS OF S&P INDEX (supplemental p.1)

This oscillator/cycle combination confirmed the bottom of EVERY 4-Year cycle. Five steps must be met:

1. The price low must be **32 or more months** from the previous 4-Year Cycle bottom
2. The Detrend must drop below the Buy line at **-70** and turn up
3. The CCI must drop below zero. A drop below the Buy Line at **-100** (which occurred at 6 of 9 bottoms) is comforting but not necessary
4. The CCI must then rise above the Crossover
5. Following requirements 1-4 being met, the **Trigger Entry** is buying the price high of the month that exceeded the high of the Crossover month — confirmed every 4-Year Cycle bottom. It is the safest entry but not always the one with lowest dollar risk.

### CCI58 OSC/CYCLE COMBO FOR TRADING CYCLE LOW IN T-BONDS (supplemental p.1)

1. Prices must be in the Trading Cycle Timing Band (**15-30 market days** from previous Trading Cycle low)
2. The CCI58 must have dropped below the Buy Line at **-25** and turned up
3. The Trigger Entry is to place a buy stop above the high of the upturn day
4. The protective stop should be placed below the price low of the Trading Cycle

### RSI3M3 OSC/CYCLE COMBO FOR BUYING TRADING CYCLE BOTTOMS IN T-BONDS (supplemental p.2)

1. The low must be **15 to 30 market days** from the previous Trading Cycle bottom
2. The Trading Cycle high must have occurred with the oscillator above the Sell Line at **60**
3. The oscillator must drop below the Buy Line at **40** and turn up before or as the price low is made
4. The Trigger Entry is a rise above the price high of the upturn day
5. The protective stop should be placed below the Trading Cycle low

### DJIA RSI3M3 OSC/CYCLE COMBO FOR PRIMARY CYCLE BOTTOM (supplemental p.2)

1. Prices are declining into the **13-20 week** trough-to-trough timing band (can extend to up to 25 weeks)
