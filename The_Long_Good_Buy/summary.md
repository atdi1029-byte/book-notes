# The Long Good Buy: Analysing Cycles in Markets
## By Peter C. Oppenheimer (2020, Wiley, 316 pages)

### One-Line Summary
A Goldman Sachs chief strategist's data-driven guide to understanding, navigating, and profiting from the recurring phases of financial market cycles.

### Core Thesis
Despite enormous changes in technology, politics, and economics over the past century, financial markets follow recognizable cyclical patterns driven by the interplay of economic fundamentals, investor psychology, and policy responses. Investors who understand these patterns — particularly the four phases of the equity cycle, the three types of bear markets, and the role of valuations in determining long-term returns — can dramatically improve their outcomes by recognizing inflection points and avoiding the worst drawdowns.

### Key Frameworks

**The Four Phases of the Equity Cycle:**
1. **Despair** (avg 16 months): Bear market. Prices fall ~43% despite modest earnings growth. Valuation-driven contraction.
2. **Hope** (avg 9 months): HIGHEST returns (~60% annualised). P/E expansion while earnings still falling. Best time to buy — but hardest psychologically.
3. **Growth** (avg 49 months): Longest phase. Earnings grow +62% but returns only +16%. P/E contracts as earnings catch up.
4. **Optimism** (avg 23 months): Second-best returns (+62%). Valuations outstrip earnings. FOMO dominates. Sets up next bear.

**Three Types of Bear Markets:**
1. **Cyclical** (avg 27 months, -31%): Normal recession bears. Rate-driven. Recover in ~4 years.
2. **Event-driven** (avg 9 months, -29%): Exogenous shocks. Shortest. Recover in ~15 months.
3. **Structural** (avg 42 months, -57%): Bubble bursts + deleveraging. Deepest. Take ~10 YEARS to recover.

**Bear Market Risk Indicator:** Six variables — ISM/unemployment, inflation, yield curve, growth momentum, valuation (CAPE), private sector financial balance.

### Key Data Points
- US equities since 1860: ~10% average annual total return across all holding periods
- Over 20-year periods: equities have NEVER had negative returns
- 75% of S&P 500 total return since 1973 came from reinvested dividends
- CAPE P/E explains 70% of subsequent 10-year returns (R² = 0.70)
- Hope phase delivers 36% of total cycle returns in just 9 months
- Structural bears take 9+ years to recover vs ~1 year for event-driven
- Post-2008: longest US economic cycle in 150+ years, driven by QE and ultra-low rates
- FAAMG traded at 25x P/E vs tech bubble top 5 at 55x — current tech is cheaper

### What Makes This Book Distinctive
- Written by a practitioner with 35 years at Goldman Sachs, not an academic
- Combines rigorous historical data (150+ years) with personal anecdotes from living through every crisis since 1985
- The four-phase cycle framework provides a practical, actionable model for positioning
- Comprehensive bear market taxonomy helps investors distinguish temporary setbacks from structural disasters
- Honest about the limits of forecasting while showing what CAN be known probabilistically
