# The Bond Book — Annette Thau

## One-Sentence Summary
A comprehensive, plain-English guide to every corner of the bond market — from Treasuries and munis to mortgage-backed securities and bond funds — written to help individual investors understand what they're buying, what risks they're taking, and how to build a bond portfolio that matches their goals.

## Key Themes
1. **Risk transparency** — every bond investment carries specific, identifiable risks (interest rate, credit, call, reinvestment, inflation); understanding them lets you control your exposure
2. **Bonds are not simple** — many bonds and bond funds are as volatile and unpredictable as stocks; "safe" labels often hide real dangers
3. **Individual empowerment** — armed with the right vocabulary and concepts, any investor can evaluate bonds, talk to brokers without being intimidated, and avoid being sold a bill of goods
4. **The yield question** — "What risks am I taking to earn a higher yield?" is the central question behind every bond purchase
5. **2008 changed everything** — the financial crisis exposed hidden risks in mortgage-backed securities, bond insurance, and supposedly safe funds

## Structure
- **Part One (Ch 1-5):** Fundamentals — bond lifecycle, how bonds trade, price volatility, bond math, the Fed
- **Part Two (Ch 6-10):** Individual securities — Treasuries, munis, corporates, mortgage-backed, international
- **Part Three (Ch 11-14):** Funds — mutual funds, money market/muni funds, taxable bond funds, ETFs/CEFs/UITs
- **Part Four (Ch 15-16):** Portfolio management — ladders, barbells, swaps, asset allocation

## Who Should Read This
Individual investors who own or are considering bonds or bond funds, especially those who find the bond market intimidating or opaque. Written for non-professionals but thorough enough to serve as a reference for years.
