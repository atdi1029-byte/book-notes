# Options as a Strategic Investment
## Lawrence G. McMillan — 5th Edition (1,070 pages)

---

### One-Paragraph Summary

"Options as a Strategic Investment" is the definitive comprehensive reference
on listed equity and index options. McMillan covers every major strategy —
from basic covered call writing and outright option buying, through ratio
writes, spreads of every variety, straddle/combination writing, synthetic
positions, LEAPS, index and futures options — and culminates in an advanced
treatment of volatility trading and the Greeks. The book's core thesis is
that options are not inherently speculative; properly deployed, they can
reduce portfolio risk, generate consistent income, and provide precisely
calibrated exposure to price movements, volatility, and time. McMillan
insists on quantitative management: every strategy has specific entry
criteria, follow-up rules, and exit thresholds. Suitability — matching
strategy complexity to investor temperament and resources — is emphasized
throughout.

---

### Key Themes

1. **Risk/reward is always a trade-off.** Higher mathematical expected
   return always comes with more complexity, margin requirements, or
   psychological stress. There is no free lunch.

2. **Delta-neutral is the practitioner's foundation.** Most neutral
   strategies (ratio writes, straddle selling) must be entered at
   delta-neutral and readjusted as the underlying moves.

3. **Implied volatility is the central trading signal.** When IV is high
   relative to historical volatility, sell options. When IV is low, buy them.
   This single principle underlies dozens of strategies.

4. **Follow-up actions matter as much as entry.** McMillan devotes
   extensive space to rolling, adjusting, and defending positions — because
   how you manage a trade determines its outcome more than the initial setup.

5. **Time decay is the enemy of buyers and the friend of sellers.** Decay
   accelerates in the last 30-45 days; strategies should be structured
   accordingly.

### Strategy Hierarchy (by risk/return)

| Tier | Strategies | Risk Level |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| Highest return | Ratio writing, ratio spreading, straddle writing, naked calls | Unlimited risk; requires margin |
| High return | T-bill + option, calendar combos, diagonal spreads | Limited to moderate |
| Moderate | Covered writes, bull/bear spreads, butterflies, calendars | Limited |
| Lowest | OTM option buying (speculative) | Limited (100% of premium) |

### Parts Overview

**Part I (Ch. 1-2): Fundamentals**
Definitions, pricing, covered call writing — the bedrock. Delta, time value,
intrinsic value, striking price intervals, expiration cycles, margin.

**Part II (Ch. 3-14): Call Strategies**
Call buying, naked writing, ratio writing, bull/bear/calendar/butterfly/ratio
spreads, backspreads, diagonal spreads.

**Part III (Ch. 15-20): Put Strategies**
Put basics, protective puts, collars, put buying with calls, straddle/combo
writing.

**Part IV (Ch. 21-27): Combining Strategies**
Synthetics, put spreads, ratio put spreads, LEAPS, straddle selling,
arbitrage (conversions, reversals, box spreads).

**Part V (Ch. 28-35): Mathematical Applications & Index/Futures Options**
Black-Scholes, historical and implied volatility, index option strategies
(hedging, overwriting, spreading), structured products, futures options.

**Part VI (Ch. 36-42): Volatility Trading & Advanced Concepts**
Volatility as a tradeable asset, measurement tools, distribution of stock
prices, volatility trading techniques, the Greeks (delta/gamma/theta/vega),
taxes, and the "best strategy" framework.

### The Most Important Formulas

- **Break-even for covered write:** Stock price − premium received
- **Delta-neutral ratio:** Shares / (option delta × 100)
- **Hedge ratio (puts needed):** (Portfolio value × Beta) / (Index × $100)
- **Black-Scholes:** C = S·N(d₁) − X·e^(−rT)·N(d₂); delta = N(d₁)
- **Historical vol (annualized):** daily std dev of log returns × √260
- **Probability of ending below K:** P = N[ln(K/S) / (σ·√T)]
- **Futures fair value:** FV = Index × (1 + r·T) − ΣDividends/(1+r)^t

### Who Should Read This

Options practitioners at any level — from covered call writers seeking to
understand assignment risk, to volatility traders needing rigorous
measurement frameworks. Not a quick read; designed as a reference to be
returned to repeatedly as the reader advances in strategy complexity.
