## Chapter 8: Silent Evidence, Luck, and the Illusion of Stability

### The Problem with Identifying Talent

- **Balzac's counterargument to merit narratives**: We attribute **Honoré de Balzac's** success to superior "realism," "insights," "sensitivity," but this logic collapses under scrutiny. Many manuscripts with similar qualities perished unpublished—Balzac was less uniquely talented than we believe, more **beneficiary of disproportionate luck**.
- **The cemetery of rejected works**: The New Yorker rejects ~100 manuscripts daily. Thousands of talented writers vanished without trace—we analyze only survivors, creating false causal narratives about their excellence. France accepts 1 in 10,000 manuscripts from first-time authors; consider actors who passed auditions but never got lucky break.
- **Bibliothèque de la Pléiade example**: Gallimard's selective literary collection validates canonical judgment, but **Balzac himself outlined success's dependence on external, cosmetic factors**—not intrinsic merit. In *Lost Illusions*, **Lucien de Rubempré**, told he is talented by the semi-aristocratic set, gets rejected by publisher who never read his work. Later the same manuscript accepted by another publisher who *also didn't read it*. The work itself remained secondary; success driven by **wile and promotion**.

### How to Become a Millionaire in Ten Steps

- **Survivorship bias in success narratives**: Studies of millionaires examine hot-shots (big titles, big jobs) and infer shared traits—courage, risk-taking, optimism—then claim these caused success. Cemetery of failed persons shares **identical traits**: luck, plain luck, is the differentiator.
- **Computational epistemology as proof**: Manufacturing random cohorts of purely random investors, simulating market performance—*just by luck*, some produce steady winners (losers fired, survivors retained). No cemetery observed; spurious narratives emerge ("He eats tofu," "She works late"). This demonstrates we need to see the cause—not just outcomes.
- **Scalable professions produce larger cemeteries**: Acting (scalable) produces far more starving aspirants than accounting, yet average earnings identical. Cemetery larger; survivorship bias more vicious.

### A Health Club for Rats

- **Hardening via ordeal**: Thought experiment—assort population of rats (fat, thin, sickly, strong) from NYC restaurants, expose to increasing radiation. Survivors naturally stronger; all weaker eliminated. Observer concludes: **treatment is excellent health-club replacement**.
- **The vicious invisibility of bias**: The more lethal the process, less visible the risk, larger difference between survivors and rest—**the more fooled the observer** about strengthening effect. One of two ingredients necessary: (a) degree of inequality in strength, or (b) unevenness/diversity in base cohort/treatment. Diversity here = uncertainty inherent in process.

### More Hidden Applications

- **Species extinction underestimated**: Number of extinct species vastly underestimated—fossils capture only tiny fraction of species that "came and left without trace." Real extinction rate ~99.5% of all species transiting earth. Life far more fragile than thought, but does NOT justify guilt for extinctions (species naturally perished before human interference).
- **Crime & invisible criminal class**: Newspapers report caught criminals; silent cemetery = uncaught tax evaders, government bribes, poisoning of wealthy spouses (undetectable substances), drug trafficking. Our representation of "standard criminal" **biased toward less intelligent ones caught**.

### The Evolution of the Swimmer's Body & Beginner's Luck

- **Beginner's luck optical illusion**: Gamblers "always lucky when they start"; same applies stock speculators. Those starting lucky (given casino has advantage) continue with destiny-selected mindset; unlucky, discouraged, stop. **Dropouts absent from surviving gamblers' community**. Explains beginner's luck.
- **Swimmer's body inverse causality**: Runners look anorexic, cyclists broken-legged, weight lifters insecure—attributed to sport selection. **Inverse truth**: genetic variance predisposes body shapes; those with natural swimmer's build *become* swimmers. Muscle grows identically whether steroids or gym climbing.

### What You See and What You Don't See

- **Katrina & invisible consequences**: Politicians promised "rebuilding" with public funds. Visible: aid to victims. Invisible: funds diverted from cancer research, diabetes prevention. Cancer patients died lonely, unmediated. More died on road (fear-driven increased driving) than in hurricane. **Money taken from unseen victims, indirectly**.
- **Bastiat's framework**: 19th-century **Frédéric Bastiat** (French humanist, independent, unknown at home, ally of **Pierre Bayle**) exposed invisible consequences of government actions. We see benefits (visible good); not alternatives (invisible good foregone).
- **Asymmetric visibility**: Job-protection measures benefit protected workers (visible, self-ascribed); harm those unable to find jobs (invisible). Cancer patients punished by Katrina aid never knew bin Laden cost them indirectly.
- **September 11 invisible toll**: ~2,500 killed in Twin Towers; ~1,000 died silently after—afraid of flying, switched to driving, increased road-death risk. No support; deaths unnoticed. **Bin Laden's invisible victims**.
- **Ralph Nader paradox**: Saved most American lives via seatbelt advocacy, yet forgot to trumpet tens of thousands saved. Easier to sell visible action than invisible prevention.

### Doctors & the Salience of Harm Over Benefit

- **Drug paradox**: Drug saves many from dangerous ailment, risks killing few. Doctor has no incentive to prescribe (lawsuit danger; lives saved invisible). **Life saved = statistic (invisible); person hurt = anecdote (salient)**.
- Risk of Black Swan equally invisible.

### Casanova: The Illusion of Personal Resilience

- **Giacomo Casanova** (self-styled **Jacques, Chevalier de Seingalt**): 18th-century adventurer, legendary seducer. Wrote *History of My Life* (12 volumes, bad French). Felt **étoile** (lucky star) rescued him from reversals—every hardship recovered by invisible hand, intrinsic property to bounce back.
- **Generalized survivorship bias**: Among colorful adventurers, many crushed; few bounced repeatedly. Survivors **believe they're indestructible**—selection bias. "Adventurers singularly blessed by destiny abound" because we hear survivors' stories.
- **Flambeur (French term)**: Extravagant bon vivant, wild speculator, risk-taker with considerable charm. Fiancé of woman (civil servant's son) spent quickly, catapulted into character-novel lifestyle (shoes, cigars, cars). She explained hardship temporary; he always came back. Recently tracked: he *hasn't* recovered from latest blow, dropped from scene.
- **New York resilience illusion**: City transcends disasters repeatedly—belief this is *intrinsic property*. **Samuel M.E.** economist quoted: "record of going through rough times, coming back stronger." But simulator: Rome, Athens, Carthage, Byzantium, Tyre, Jericho, Peoria, NYC. Some survive; others perish. **Survivor is unqualified witness** to survival mechanics.

### "I Am a Risk Taker"

- **Restaurant business paradox**: Foolish to open restaurant in NYC (enormous risks). Cemetery silent. Yet people open blindly, "risk-taking genes" encouraging uncalculated risk. Economic growth comes from risk-taking; but **fool's argument**: if cautious, no spectacular growth. We inherited risk-taking genes; fools who survived.
- **Uninformed vs. informed risk**: Critique is of *uninformed* risk-taking. **Danny Kahneman**: we take risks from ignorance & blindness to probability, not bravado.
- **Evolutionary fitness reframed**: "Optimistic bent supposed to be good for us"—ancestors took challenges. But **that we survived by accident does NOT mean we should continue same risks**. Mature enough to realize: got here by accident; preserve by becoming conservative.
- **Black Swans in Extremistan**: More unfamiliar with randomness, more belief in evolution's optimality. Silent evidence absent from evolutionary theory; you see only good. Risk-taking made species head to extinction. Foolish risk-takers win short-term; in **Extremistan**, also win long-term.

### I Am a Black Swan: The Anthropic Bias

- **Self-sampling assumption collapses under scrutiny**: Odds of parameters calibrated for existence astronomically low. But **our presence completely vitiates computation**: we are surviving Casanova. Among many adventurous Casanovas, one remains kicking (by accident); odds not low: *there are so many*.
- **Reference-point argument**: Do not compute odds from vantage point of winning gambler (or lucky Casanova, invincible Carthage), but from *all who started in cohort*. Population of beginning gamblers: one will show stellar results just by luck. From reference point of beginning cohort: not a big deal. From reference point of winner: extraordinary.
- **Multiverse analogy**: Assume history delivers "bleak" (unfavorable) or "rosy" (favorable) scenarios. Bleak → extinction. Clearly, if I'm writing now, rosy scenario occurred—avoided massacre, benefited from absent war, free of meteorite/nuclear war/epidemic. Probing own biography: returned to Lebanon age 18 during war, felt extraordinary fatigue. Typhoid fever. Had antibiotics not been discovered decades earlier, wouldn't be here. Later "cured" of severe disease via recent medical technology. Alive in Internet age, reached audience—all accidental. Result of human race's rise, itself accidental.

### The Cosmetic Because

- **Weakened causality under survival conditions**: Notion of "because" severely weakened when survival in play. Aristotelian "because" assumes solid link; silent evidence masks this. Whenever survival in play, very notion of "because" deeply weakened.
- **Why didn't bubonic plague kill more?** Observers supply cosmetic explanations. But weakened-causality argument: if plague had killed more, we wouldn't be here to observe. Don't immediately look for causes; condition on survival: one cannot read effects backward-looking into process, must invoke randomness.
- **Educational system shames ignorance**: Forces students to squeeze explanations, shames "I don't know." But all survival-conditional examples (Cold War end, Persians lose Salamis, Hannibal's reversals, Casanova bounce-back) should teach invoking randomness—randomness ≠ ignorance; randomness = inability to identify cause from effects.
- **Accept "because" fuzziness**: Not saying causes don't exist; saying *not so simple*. Be suspicious of "because," handle with care—**particularly where suspected silent evidence**.
