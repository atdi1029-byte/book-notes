# Your Money or Your Life — Summary
## By Vicki Robin and Joe Dominguez (2018 revised edition, 311 pages)

### Core Thesis
Money is something you trade your life energy for. By making this exchange conscious — tracking every dollar as hours of your life — you can transform your relationship with money, achieve "enough," and ultimately reach Financial Independence: the point where passive investment income exceeds your expenses, making paid employment optional.

### The 9-Step Program

**Step 1: Making Peace with the Past** — Calculate total lifetime earnings and current net worth. Confront the gap between what you've earned and what you have to show for it.

**Step 2: Tracking Your Life Energy** — Calculate your real hourly wage (subtract all job-related costs from pay, add all job-related hours). Track every cent that enters or leaves your life.

**Step 3: Monthly Tabulation** — Categorize all spending. Convert dollars to hours of life energy. Ask: is this how I want to spend my life?

**Step 4: Three Transformative Questions** — For each spending category: (1) Did I receive fulfillment proportional to life energy spent? (2) Is this aligned with my values? (3) How would this change if I didn't need to work for money?

**Step 5: The Wall Chart** — Plot monthly income and expenses on a visible chart. The visual feedback loop drives behavioral change over time.

**Step 6: Minimize Spending** — Creative frugality (not deprivation). Ten Sure Ways to Save Money. Frugality = enjoying the virtue of getting good value for every minute of life energy.

**Step 7: Maximize Income** — Break the link between work and wages. The only purpose of paid employment is getting paid. Seek highest pay consistent with health and integrity.

**Step 8: The Crossover Point** — Plot monthly investment income (capital × interest rate / 12) as a third line. When it crosses the expense line = Financial Independence. Three pillars: Capital, Cushion, Cache.

**Step 9: Investing for FI** — Conservative, long-term investing. Originally Treasury bonds (Joe's era). Now primarily low-cost index funds (the FIRE approach). Also: real estate, SRI, local lending, side hustles.

### Key Concepts
- **Life energy** — Money represents irretrievable hours of your life
- **Fulfillment curve** — More spending increases happiness only up to "enough," then declines
- **Real hourly wage** — True pay after subtracting all job costs (commuting, wardrobe, decompression, etc.)
- **The Crossover Point** — When monthly investment income exceeds monthly expenses
- **25x Rule** — Need 25 times annual expenses to be financially independent (4% safe withdrawal rate)
- **Capital, Cushion, Cache** — Three layers of financial security
- **ABCs of Natural Wealth** — Abilities, Belonging, Community
- **Financial Interdependence** — Beyond independence, the relational economy of giving and receiving
- **No shame, no blame** — The mantra for examining your money life without judgment

### Thematic Threads
1. **Money as life energy** — The foundational reframe that makes every financial decision existential
2. **Enough** — The peak of the fulfillment curve, neither poverty nor excess
3. **Breaking the work-wages link** — Redefining work as any purposeful activity, not just paid employment
4. **Frugality as joy** — Latin roots: frugality means enjoying the virtue of getting value
5. **Consciousness over tips** — The transformation, not the tips, saves money
6. **Community and planet** — Spending less = benefiting others and the environment
7. **The FIRE movement** — Modern inheritors of the program (Mr. Money Mustache, J.L. Collins, Bogleheads)
