# The Muscle and Strength Pyramid: Training
## Eric Helms, Andy Morgan, Andrea Valdez (2nd Ed., 2019)

**Core premise:** Training variables aren't created equal. Helms
organizes them into a six-level hierarchy — the pyramid — where
the base matters most and the apex matters least. Master the
fundamentals before obsessing over the details.

---

## The Pyramid (bottom to top)

1. **Adherence** — If you can't sustain it, nothing else matters.
   A suboptimal program you follow beats an optimal program you
   abandon. The REF framework: Realistic, Enjoyable, Flexible.

2. **Volume, Intensity, Frequency** — The three interdependent
   levers of training. Volume: 10–20 sets per muscle per week.
   Intensity: most work in the 6–12 rep range (hypertrophy) or
   1–6 rep range (strength), RPE 5–10. Frequency: at least 2×
   per muscle per week.

3. **Progression** — How the stimulus increases over time. Novices
   progress session-to-session (linear). Intermediates week-to-week
   (wave loading). Advanced athletes month-to-month (block
   periodization: accumulation → intensification → realization).

4. **Exercise Selection** — More important for strength than
   hypertrophy. Powerlifters: 50–75% competition lifts. Bodybuilders:
   1–2 compounds + 1–3 isolations per muscle. Full ROM. Compound
   lifts first. Build expertise in your movements.

5. **Rest Periods** — The "hormone hypothesis" is debunked. Longer
   rest (2.5+ min for compounds, 1.5+ min for isolations) produces
   equal or better results. Antagonist paired sets save time without
   sacrificing performance.

6. **Lifting Tempo** — The least important variable. Control the
   eccentric, accelerate the concentric. Intentionally slow training
   reduces volume and impulse. Maximal intent doubles strength gains
   vs. deliberate slow concentric.

## Key Science

- Volume sweet spot: 10–20 sets/muscle/week; past ~20 sets returns
  diminish. Gonzalez-Badillo (2005): moderate volume > high or low.
- RPE/RIR scale (Tuchscherer/Helms/Zourdos): RPE 10 = failure,
  RPE 8 = 2 reps left, RPE 7 = 3 reps left.
- Fatigue dissipates faster than fitness → why deloads work.
- Full ROM superior to partial for hypertrophy (multiple studies).
- Mind-muscle connection effective only below ~80% 1RM.
- Frequency 2×/week per muscle superior to 1×/week for hypertrophy
  (Schoenfeld 2016 meta-analysis).

## Who This Book Is For

Anyone who wants to stop following programs blindly and start
building programs intelligently. The goal is understanding the
principles so deeply that you can design, adjust, and troubleshoot
your own training. Includes full sample programs at novice,
intermediate, and advanced levels for both powerlifting and
bodybuilding.
