# The Man Who Solved the Market
## How Jim Simons Launched the Quant Revolution
### Gregory Zuckerman (2019)

**One-line summary:** The story of how mathematician Jim Simons built Renaissance Technologies and its Medallion fund — the most successful hedge fund in history — by hiring scientists instead of traders and trusting algorithms over human intuition.

## Key Points

- Jim Simons, a world-class geometer and former Cold War codebreaker, left academia at forty to build a trading firm that would use mathematical models instead of traditional Wall Street methods
- After a decade of painful failures with partners Lenny Baum and James Ax, Simons assembled the right team: Elwyn Berlekamp pioneered short-term trading, Henry Laufer built the "betting algorithm," and IBM recruits Peter Brown and Robert Mercer automated the stock-trading system
- The Medallion fund averaged 66% annual returns before fees from 1988 to 2019 — generating over $100 billion in trading profits with just 300 employees
- Renaissance's edge came from hiring mathematicians, physicists, and linguists who treated markets as a pattern-recognition problem rather than a financial one, combined with a single monolithic trading system where all researchers shared code
- The book traces the human costs of extraordinary wealth: Simons lost two sons, his partnership with Baum ended in acrimony, and co-CEO Robert Mercer's political activities (bankrolling Breitbart, Steve Bannon, and Donald Trump's 2016 campaign) nearly tore the firm apart
- Simons pioneered the quant revolution that by 2019 saw algorithmic traders control a third of all stock-market activity
