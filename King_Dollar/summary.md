# King Dollar — Summary

**Author:** Paul Blustein
**Published:** 2025, Yale University Press
**Pages:** ~290

## One-Paragraph Summary

Paul Blustein, a veteran financial journalist based in Japan, delivers the most comprehensive and up-to-date account of the US dollar's extraordinary global dominance — how it achieved its throne, why no rival comes close to unseating it, and the profound responsibilities that come with wielding such power. Drawing on the Spider-Man adage ("with great power comes great responsibility"), Blustein traces the dollar's journey from the chaotic porters of 1850s Wall Street through the creation of the Fed, Bretton Woods, the Nixon Shock, and the 2008 financial crisis, then turns to the modern weaponization of dollar-based financial infrastructure (SWIFT, CHIPS, OFAC sanctions) against Iran, North Korea, and Russia. He systematically demolishes the pretenders — the euro (structurally flawed), the renminbi (capital controls and authoritarian risk), Bitcoin (failed as money), and CBDCs (overhyped) — while warning that America's greatest threat to the dollar comes not from foreign rivals but from its own policy missteps: sanctions overuse, crypto-industry capture of politics, fiscal recklessness, attacks on Fed independence, and the terrifying prospect of sanctioning China over Taiwan.

## Key Themes

1. **The dollar's dominance rests on institutional foundations, not government decree** — rule of law, deep capital markets, Fed credibility, network effects, and historical incumbency
2. **Weaponization is a double-edged sword** — sanctions against Iran succeeded brilliantly; against Russia, they revealed the limits of financial coercion against large commodity-exporting economies
3. **No rival currency is remotely close** — the euro lacks fiscal union, the renminbi lacks openness and trust, crypto lacks stability, CBDCs are politically toxic
4. **The real danger is American self-sabotage** — sanctions overuse, Trump-era crypto politics, fiscal irresponsibility, erosion of institutional credibility
5. **"With great power comes great responsibility"** — the Spider-Man metaphor runs throughout: the dollar's power must be exercised with restraint, or the curses against King Dollar will be justified
