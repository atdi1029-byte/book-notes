# The 7 Deadly Innocent Frauds of Economic Policy — Summary

**Author:** Warren Mosler | **Year:** 2010 | **Pages:** 114

## What This Book Is About
A hedge fund manager who made hundreds of millions in bond markets explains why almost everything politicians, economists, and the media say about government debt, deficits, taxes, Social Security, and trade is wrong — not because they're lying, but because they genuinely don't understand how modern money actually works. The book is the accessible version of Modern Monetary Theory (MMT), grounded in Mosler's real-world experience in banking and finance, not academic theory.

## The Core Insight
The U.S. government is the issuer of the dollar, not a user of it. It cannot "run out" of dollars any more than a bowling alley can run out of points. Government spending is just changing numbers in bank accounts — data entry. This means:
- The government never needs to "get" dollars from taxes or borrowing before it spends
- Government checks cannot bounce (operationally)
- The only real constraint is inflation (too much spending power chasing too few goods), not solvency
- Taxes exist to regulate spending power and create demand for the currency, not to "fund" the government
- Government securities (Treasuries) are just savings accounts at the Fed — not "borrowing"

## The Seven Frauds

| # | The Fraud | The Reality |
|---|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | Government must tax or borrow to spend | Government spends by changing numbers in accounts. It must spend FIRST before it can tax (people need dollars to pay taxes) |
| 2 | Deficits burden our children | Children will consume what they produce. You can't send goods back in time. Real burden = unemployment and lost productivity TODAY |
| 3 | Deficits take away savings | Deficits ADD to savings, dollar for dollar (accounting identity). Surpluses DRAIN savings — 6 surplus periods in US history → 6 depressions |
| 4 | Social Security is broken | Government checks don't bounce. The "trust fund" is just record-keeping. Real issue is productivity (dependency ratio), not money |
| 5 | Trade deficit is unsustainable | Imports = real benefits, exports = real costs. Japan sends us 2M cars/year and gets a bank statement. They think they're winning? |
| 6 | We need savings to fund investment | Investment creates savings (not the reverse). "Savings is the accounting record of investment." Forced savings programs are demand leakages |
| 7 | Higher deficits today = higher taxes tomorrow | Yes, and that's a GOOD thing — it means the economy recovered enough to need cooling down |

## Key Themes

### Money as a Spreadsheet
Modern money is not a physical thing. When the government spends, it adds numbers to bank accounts. When it taxes, it marks them down. A Treasury security is just a savings account at the Fed. "Paying off the national debt" means moving numbers from savings accounts to checking accounts. The $13 trillion national debt is just the total in savings accounts at the Fed.

### The Parent-Children Analogy
Parents create coupons, pay children for chores, tax them 10 per week. The parents can never "run out" of coupons. They must SPEND coupons first before children can earn them to pay the tax. If parents pay 2 coupons for the same chore they used to pay 1, the value of each coupon drops by half — that's inflation.

### Taxes Create Currency Demand
The British "hut tax" in colonial Africa: impose a tax payable only in British coins → instantly people need British coins → they offer labor and goods for sale for coins → the colony is monetized. Same principle applies to the US dollar. Taxes drive demand for the currency.

### The Real Constraint: Inflation
The book is NOT saying the government can spend without limit. Over-spending drives up prices. The right deficit is whatever level keeps unemployment low without causing inflation. Taxes are the throttle — they regulate spending power.

## Mosler's Biography (Part II)
The book includes Mosler's autobiography from Manchester CT savings bank through Wall Street, revealing how each career step taught him something about how money really works:
- **Savings Bank of Manchester (1973):** Loan collections taught him credit enforcement drives currency value. Arbitraged GNMA delivery dates — first fixed-income MBS arbitrage. Salomon Brothers called him to understand the trade.
- **Bache, Bankers Trust (1975-1980s):** Wall Street. Pioneered derivative products. Understood reserve accounting when senior economists didn't. Colonial Mortgage default taught him about corporate blame.
- **William Blair & Company (1978-1982):** Fixed income arbitrage in Chicago. 10-year note futures trade made ~$3M in weeks. Bond/futures arbitrage during Hunt brothers silver crisis — held through $1M paper loss, backed by Ned Janotta.
- **Illinois Income Investors (1982-1997):** Founded with Justin Adams and Cliff Viner. 20-year track record: one losing month ever, #1 risk-adjusted returns worldwide, $3.5B capital.
- **The JBU6 Trade (1996):** Bought more of Japan's cheapest deliverable bond than Japan had issued. Dealers had to beg him to borrow bonds they'd sold short. Made ~$150 million. Largest futures delivery of all time.
- **Italian Epiphany (early 1990s):** Italy's debt/GDP 110%, academics predicted default. Mosler realized: a sovereign issuing its own nonconvertible floating-rate currency CANNOT default. Met with Italian Treasury official Luigi Spaventa who confirmed the insight. Made $100M+ on Italian bonds. This experience crystallized all 7 frauds into a coherent framework.

## Policy Proposals (Part III)
1. **Full payroll tax holiday** — suspend all FICA/Medicare deductions → $650+/month for average couple
2. **$500/capita revenue sharing to states** — immediate $150B unrestricted transfer
3. **$8/hr national service job** for anyone willing to work — with full benefits, acts as "labor buffer stock"
4. **Universal health care** — $5,000/person/year in a bank account, unspent = cash rebate, above $5,000 = Medicare-like coverage
5. **Monetary system reforms** — Fed lend unsecured, remove FDIC cap, eliminate interbank market, Treasury fund housing agencies directly, 0% fed funds rate, stop issuing securities longer than 90 days
6. **Strategic stockpiles** — extend beyond oil to food and necessities
7. **Housing foreclosure plan** — govt buys house at lower of market/mortgage value, rents to former owner, resells after 2 years
