Who is Claude Shannon and why is he important?	Father of information theory. His 1948 paper "A Mathematical Theory of Communication" proved that information is measurable in bits. His master's thesis linked Boolean algebra to electrical circuits, becoming the foundation of digital computing. Also built the first wearable computer (to beat roulette) and achieved 28% annual stock returns over 35 years.	Fortune's_Formula people
What is a "bit" in information theory?	The fundamental unit of information — the answer to a single yes/no question. Coined by Claude Shannon in 1948. A fair coin flip contains 1 bit of entropy; a biased coin (90% heads) contains less.	Fortune's_Formula concepts
What is entropy in Shannon's information theory?	A measure of uncertainty. Formula: H = -Sum of p(i) * log2(p(i)). High entropy = high uncertainty = more information needed to resolve it. Shannon borrowed the term from thermodynamics on the advice of John von Neumann.	Fortune's_Formula concepts
What is the Kelly Criterion?	A formula for optimal bet sizing: Bet size = edge / odds. It maximizes long-term compound growth rate, never risks total ruin (bets shrink as you lose), and reaches any wealth target faster than any other strategy.	Fortune's_Formula concepts
Who was John Kelly Jr.?	Bell Labs physicist who published the optimal betting formula in 1956. Proved that a gambler with a private information source can compound wealth at a rate equal to the information transmission rate (G_max = R). Died of a brain hemorrhage at age 41 while walking to a meeting at IBM.	Fortune's_Formula people
What is "fractional Kelly" and why do people use it?	Betting half (or less) of the full Kelly amount. Full Kelly is volatile — you'll frequently lose half your bankroll before recovering. Fractional Kelly sacrifices modest long-term return for dramatically smoother ride and smaller drawdowns.	Fortune's_Formula concepts
Who was Ed Thorp?	MIT math PhD who applied information theory to gambling and then finance. Invented card counting for blackjack (published "Beat the Dealer" in 1962). Co-founded Princeton-Newport Partners with Jay Regan — 19 years of positive returns averaging 15.1% annually. Also invented delta hedging for options.	Fortune's_Formula people

Russia's 1998 default turned their "diversified" bets into correlated losses. Lost 90% of value, required a $3.6 billion Fed-organized bailout. They were operating at roughly 2x Kelly-optimal leverage.	Fortune's_Formula events
What is the core lesson of the LTCM blowup through the Kelly lens?	Position sizing matters as much as having an edge. LTCM had genuine mathematical insight but overbetting by ~2x Kelly guarantees eventual ruin. Kelly's theory predicts that betting 2x optimal will eventually produce a compound return of zero, no matter how good your strategy.	Fortune's_Formula concepts

How did Thorp's blackjack card counting work?	Track the ratio of high cards (tens, aces) to low cards remaining in the deck. When the deck is rich in high cards, the player has an advantage — bet more. When it's poor, bet minimum. Proved mathematically with IBM computer simulations. $10,000 became $21,000 in 30 hours of play.	Fortune's_Formula concepts
What is the connection between Shannon's information theory and Kelly's gambling formula?	They're the same mathematics. Shannon proved information (reduction of uncertainty) is measurable in bits. Kelly showed a gambler with an information edge can compound wealth at a rate exactly equal to the rate of information flow. The gambling formula IS information theory applied to betting. What was the St. Petersburg Paradox and how does it relate to the Kelly debate?	A theoretical coin-flip game with infinite expected value (pot doubles each flip) that no rational person would pay infinite money to play. Daniel Bernoulli resolved it in 1738 by introducing utility theory and the geometric mean — you should maximize the geometric mean of wealth, not the arithmetic mean. This is essentially the Kelly Criterion 200 years early.	Fortune's_Formula concepts

What is the central argument of Fortune's Formula?	Information theory provides a unified language for understanding risk across all domains — telegraph wires, roulette wheels, blackjack tables, stock markets, bond spreads. What does "the line between gambling and investing is a minus sign" mean?	Favorable bets (positive expected value) are investments. Unfavorable bets (negative expected value) are gambles. The math is identical — only the sign of the edge differs. This was the core insight connecting Shannon, Kelly, and Thorp's work across casinos and Wall Street.	Fortune's_Formula themes
Why is secrecy essential to maintaining an information edge?	Once a profitable strategy becomes public knowledge, the market adapts and the advantage disappears. This is why Thorp kept his warrant-pricing discoveries secret, why Shannon never published his stock strategy, and why the wire service monopoly was worth a fortune — exclusive access to information IS the edge. What did Thorp discover about warrants that led to Wall Street?	Thorp found that warrants (options to buy stock at a set price) were systematically mispriced by the market. He developed delta hedging — offsetting warrant positions with stock positions to lock in profit regardless of market direction. This was the precursor to the Black-Scholes options pricing model. What is Shannon's channel capacity theorem?	Every communication channel has a maximum rate for reliable information transmission. Revolutionary finding: you can get arbitrarily close to error-free communication by clever encoding, even in noisy environments. This enabled everything from deep-space probes to digital cell phones.	Fortune's_Formula concepts
What three properties make the Kelly Criterion optimal?	1) Maximizes long-term compound growth rate of wealth. 2) Never risks total ruin (bets are proportional to current wealth, so they shrink as you lose). 3) Reaches any target wealth level faster than any other strategy. How is overbetting worse than underbetting in Kelly theory?	Underbetting (below Kelly) sacrifices some growth but is still profitable. Overbetting (above Kelly) is catastrophic — at 2x Kelly, your long-run compound return approaches zero. LTCM operated at roughly 2x Kelly leverage, which is why their blowup was inevitable regardless of their mathematical sophistication.	Fortune's_Formula concepts
