# The Prize: The Epic Quest for Oil, Money & Power
## Daniel Yergin (1991) — Pulitzer Prize Winner

### Overview
A sweeping narrative history of the oil industry from its origins in 1850s Pennsylvania through Iraq's 1990 invasion of Kuwait. Yergin traces three interwoven themes: oil as the foundation of modern capitalism, oil as a weapon of geopolitics and war, and oil as the substance that created "Hydrocarbon Man" — the oil-dependent civilization we inhabit. The cast spans wildcatters and tycoons, dictators and prime ministers, scientists and spies — from Rockefeller to Saddam Hussein, Churchill to Khomeini.

### Key Themes

**1. Oil and Capitalism**
The oil industry birthed the modern corporation. Rockefeller's Standard Oil was among the first multinationals; its breakup spawned the companies that would dominate the 20th century. The story repeats: boom-bust cycles, monopoly vs. competition, the tension between state control and private enterprise, from Baku to Venezuela to the North Sea.

**2. Oil and War**
Every major 20th-century conflict was shaped by petroleum. Churchill converted the Royal Navy from coal to oil before WWI. Hitler invaded Russia for Caucasus oil. Japan attacked Pearl Harbor because America cut off its petroleum supply. The Allies "floated to victory on a wave of oil." Patton's tanks ran dry at the Meuse. The 1973 embargo became the "oil weapon." Iraq invaded Kuwait for its reserves.

**3. Oil and Civilization**
Kerosene pushed back the night; gasoline created suburban America. Oil provides fertilizer, plastics, transportation — the infrastructure of modern life. The environmental movement challenges this dependency, yet "Hydrocarbon Man shows little inclination to give up his cars."

### The Arc of Power
- **1859-1911**: American dominance — Drake's well through Standard Oil's monopoly and dissolution
- **1911-1945**: Global struggle — Royal Dutch/Shell, Persian oil, two world wars fought over petroleum
- **1945-1970**: Postwar order — Seven Sisters, fifty-fifty deals, Middle East becomes center of gravity
- **1970-1986**: OPEC revolution — embargo, price shocks, the greatest transfer of wealth in history, then collapse
- **1990**: Full circle — Iraq invades Kuwait, "mastery itself was once more the prize"

### Essential Insight
Oil is never just a commodity. It is always entangled with sovereignty, military power, and the fate of nations. Every era produces the illusion that oil has been tamed — by technology, markets, or diplomacy — and every era is eventually reminded otherwise. The minister asked how long the lessons would last replied: "About three years, without reminding." Within a year, his country was invaded.
