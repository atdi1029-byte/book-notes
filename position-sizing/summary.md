# Van Tharp's Definitive Guide to Position Sizing Strategies

## Published

## Core Thesis
Position sizing — the algorithm that determines "how much" to trade per position — is responsible for roughly 90% of trading system performance, yet it receives almost no attention compared to entries and indicators. The purpose of position sizing is not to maximize returns but to **meet your specific objectives** while managing the risk of ruin. Even a mediocre system can succeed with proper position sizing; even a superb system will fail without it.

## Key Frameworks

### R-Multiples
All trades expressed as multiples of initial risk (R). Buy at $50 with a $45 stop = $5 risk per share = 1R. A $15 profit = 3R. This normalization allows comparison across any instrument or timeframe.

### System Quality Number (SQN)
SQN = (Expectancy / Std Dev of R) x sqrt(N). Mathematically equivalent to a t-score testing whether expectancy differs significantly from zero. Rating scale: <1.0 = hard to trade, 1.7+ = statistically significant, 3.0+ = excellent, 5.0+ = superb, 7.0+ = Holy Grail.

### CPR Formula
P = C / R. Cash at risk (C) = % of equity x account size. Risk per unit (R) = distance to stop x unit size. Position size (P) = number of units. Simple, universal, applicable to any market.

### 31 Position Sizing Models
Organized into three categories:
- **Core models (1-5):** Fixed units, equal leverage, percent margin, percent volatility, percent risk
- **Advanced models (6-22):** Group control, portfolio heat, scaling in/out, market's money, FRPS, SQN-based ruin avoidance, two-tier, Basso-Schwager rebalancing
- **Models to avoid (23-31):** Martingale variants, Kelly criterion, optimal f, intuitive sizing

## Key Insight
In a test using random entries with a volatility-based trailing stop, position sizing alone caused final equity to vary from $382K to $640M — a 1,700x difference — using the same entry signals and exits. The "how much" decision dwarfs the "what to buy" decision.
